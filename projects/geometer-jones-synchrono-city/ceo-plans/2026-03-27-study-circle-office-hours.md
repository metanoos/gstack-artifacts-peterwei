## Vision

### 10x Check
The 10x version of this product is not a nicer church website. It is a map-native trust ladder for real-world rooms. A curious person opens one circle, understands exactly what kind of room it is, sees whether the host is actually present, joins quietly with low social risk, gets enough signal to decide whether this is their kind of room, then shows up in person. One organizer can run multiple distinct circles without the product collapsing them into one audience blob. Later, many organizers on their own infrastructure can do the same.

## Scope Decisions

| # | Proposal | Effort | Decision | Reasoning |
|---|----------|--------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | First-timer trust mode as a separate feature | S | SKIPPED | The trust ladder should be default product behavior, not a bolted-on mode. |
| 2 | Soft RSVP / intent-to-attend | S | ACCEPTED | This creates the cleanest learning loop from online sampling to offline attendance. |
| 3 | Per-session archive and summaries | S | ACCEPTED | Session memory is core to trust, seriousness, and room continuity. |
| 4 | Host-level multi-circle index | M | DEFERRED | Too early. It risks building a parallel directory before the room-level wedge is proven. |
| 5 | Calendar export / reminder actions | S | ACCEPTED | High-leverage polish that helps curiosity become presence with little architectural cost. |

## Accepted Scope (added to this plan)
- Keep the beacon as the public spatial anchor and add a thin Concierge-backed layer for circle schedule, state, and memory.
- Add soft RSVP or intent-to-attend for upcoming circle sessions.
- Add per-session archive records with summaries and next-step context.
- Add calendar export or reminder actions from the circle surface.
- Bake first-timer trust defaults into the baseline UX: join muted, clear norms, easy exit, no accidental pressure to perform.
