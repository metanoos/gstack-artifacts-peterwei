# Design: Singular focus for AI-native writing auditions

Generated by /office-hours on 2026-07-16
Branch: codex/headless-press
Repo: metanoos/zine
Status: APPROVED
Mode: Startup

## Problem Statement

Zine currently mixes three meanings across its directory, panels, author
gestures, and trace player:

1. The trace a person is working on or inspecting.
2. The directory items collected for a move, delete, or context-menu action.
3. The trace or traces loaded into replay.

The overlap makes Step, Send, Attest, and replay appear to follow focus in the
common file case while still routing through selection state in edge cases.
Folders and Coins are especially inconsistent. This is not acceptable for the
first commercial workflow, where a reviewer must understand exactly which
candidate trace is being assessed and which version is historical.

The product needs one singular focus that selects the subject of author
gestures and replay. Directory selection must become a separate operation set.

## Demand Evidence

Direct demand for Zine is not yet validated. The project is pre-product, and no
potential customer has completed an uncoached workflow.

Category evidence supports testing the wedge:

- Gartner reported in 2025 that 39% of surveyed candidates used AI during the
  application process, including 36% who used it for a writing sample.
- WGU reported in 2025 that 52% of surveyed employers assess AI competency
  through skills-based assessments or on-project evaluation.
- Indeed recommends role-relevant writing samples for writing-heavy jobs and
  recommends asking candidates to explain their process.
- Grammarly Authorship demonstrates demand for full writing-process replay in
  education.
- HiringTest.ai demonstrates emerging demand for evaluating how candidates use
  AI, although it emphasizes automated scoring and identity checks.

These signals validate the problem category, not Zine's product. The next
validation must be behavioral.

## Status Quo

Content teams currently combine portfolios, take-home writing samples, live
interviews, Google Docs history, screen sharing, and subjective follow-up
questions. Technical teams can ask candidates to commit every change, but that
workflow is manual, unfamiliar to most writers, and does not naturally preserve
model identity, prompts, cited context, per-passage authorship, or the difference
between a private checkpoint, a discussion version, and an endorsed version.

The status quo produces polished output but weak evidence about the candidate's
judgment. Attempts to close the gap often become surveillance or unreliable AI
detection.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

The first buyer is the owner, content director, or editorial lead of a
small-to-mid-sized agency that repeatedly hires freelance writers.

The first workflow is a paid, AI-permitted take-home writing audition with a
24-hour submission window:

1. The agency supplies a realistic brief that contains no live client work.
2. The candidate writes asynchronously in Zine during the 24-hour window and
   may use any AI tools allowed by the brief.
3. Zine records signed Steps, model and human contributions, sources, and
   revisions.
4. The candidate Sends and Attests one exact result.
5. A human reviewer replays the process and discusses the candidate's choices,
   including rejected directions and substantive edits.

Zine evaluates judgment with AI. It does not claim to prove AI-free authorship,
detect cheating, or automatically score employability.

## Constraints

- Preserve the live working tree and its existing replay implementation.
- Do not change the trace wire format or protocol semantics.
- Keep protocol `focus` deltas as panel-occupancy observations. The new UI
  focus state is an application concept and must not create Steps.
- Files, folders, and Coins must share one focus model.
- Historical replay must be visibly read-only and must not silently redirect a
  live author gesture.
- Directory batch operations must remain efficient, including multi-select,
  drag-and-drop, rename, move, delete, and context menus.
- The focus model must work with multiple panels and duplicate open tabs.
- The audition must survive ordinary close, reopen, and crash-recovery flows
  during its 24-hour window without losing draft work or changing focus to a
  different trace.

## Premises

1. The first commercial wedge is an AI-native writing audition for content
   agencies and editorial teams.
2. AI is explicitly allowed and evaluated rather than treated as contraband.
3. A human reviewer interprets replay. Zine avoids automated hiring judgments
   and surveillance.
4. One singular focus drives author gestures and trace replay. Directory
   selection drives neither.
5. Replay is a time lens over the focused subject, not another selection
   system.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Derive focus from the active tab

Treat the focused panel's front tab as focus. Opening a directory file, Coin,
or folder makes it that tab. Replay and author gestures read from the tab.

- Effort: small
- Risk: medium
- Completeness: 6/10
- Advantage: smallest change and maximum reuse of `activePanel` and
  `PanelState.active`.
- Rejected because inline Coins, duplicate tabs, and folder replay still need
  exceptions, leaving focus inferred across several state containers.

### Approach B: Explicit singular focus

Add one authoritative UI focus state for a file, Coin, or folder. Tabs and
directory rows set it. Gold indicators render it. Author gestures and replay
consume it. Directory selection becomes independent neutral operation state.

- Effort: medium
- Risk: low-to-medium
- Completeness: 9/10
- Reuses `PanelState`, folder tabs, trace references, replay timelines,
  replay-frozen behavior, and existing focus styling.
- Chosen because it fixes the state model without replacing replay
  presentation before customer validation.

### Approach C: Dedicated replay stage

Use explicit focus, but render replay inside the focused tab's body instead of
creating replay-owned tabs and panels. A folder tab becomes a composite replay
canvas containing its historical panel layout.

- Effort: large
- Risk: medium-high
- Completeness: 10/10
- Advantage: cleanest long-term reviewer experience.
- Deferred because it replaces much of the current replay panel architecture
  before the commercial wedge has been tested.

## Recommended Approach

Implement Approach B around one rule:

> Focus chooses the subject. The player chooses a moment within that subject.
> Selection chooses directory items for an operation.

![Approved rough interaction sketch](/tmp/gstack-sketch-zine-focus.png)

### State model

Use names that distinguish UI focus from protocol focus observations:

```ts
type FocusRef =
  | { kind: "file"; path: string; nodeId?: string }
  | { kind: "folder"; path: string; nodeId?: string }
  | { kind: "coin"; nodeId: string; path?: string; phrase?: string };

type UiFocus = {
  trace: FocusRef;
  panelIndex: number;
  tabPath: string;
};

type DirectorySelection = {
  paths: ReadonlySet<string>;
  anchorPath?: string;
};
```

`UiFocus.trace` is the semantic subject. `panelIndex` and `tabPath` identify the
one visible tab that receives the gold top border. This matters when the same
trace is open in more than one panel. The directory gold bar derives from the
semantic trace path, so only the corresponding directory item is marked.

Mutable file and folder identity follows path and existing trace identity. A
new Step refreshes `nodeId` without changing focus. Coin identity follows its
immutable node id.

The existing editor text range remains separate from directory selection. It
may change the focused file's primary author gesture from Step to Mint or
Preserve, but it never retargets another trace.

### Directory interaction rules

- Primary-clicking a file, Coin, or folder label opens it in the focused panel,
  makes its tab active, sets `UiFocus`, and clears stale directory selection.
- Folders open as real folder tabs. Their chevron remains the separate control
  for expand and collapse.
- Focusing does not add the item to `DirectorySelection`.
- Cmd/Ctrl-click toggles an item's membership in `DirectorySelection` without
  opening it or moving focus.
- Shift-click extends `DirectorySelection` from its anchor without moving
  focus.
- Escape clears directory selection without moving focus.
- Right-clicking an already selected item preserves the selected set and opens
  a context menu for that set.
- Right-clicking an unselected item replaces the operation set with that one
  item while leaving focus unchanged.
- Dragging a selected item drags the selected set. Dragging an unselected item
  drags only that item. Drag start does not move focus.
- A normal primary click clears an old operation set so a later destructive
  action cannot accidentally apply to stale invisible state.

Focus uses the gold visual language. Directory selection uses a neutral gray
fill, outline, or check. Selection never borrows gold.

### Tab interaction rules

- Clicking a live tab makes its panel active, makes the tab frontmost, sets
  `UiFocus`, and clears directory selection.
- Opening a directory item and clicking its existing tab call the same focus
  transition. There is no separate selection-based routing path.
- Only the exact focused tab instance gets the gold top border. Duplicate tabs
  for the same trace do not all appear focused.
- Moving or reordering a background tab does not move focus.
- Closing the focused tab focuses the deterministic post-close active tab in
  the same panel. If that panel becomes empty, focus falls to the nearest live
  panel's active tab. If no live tab remains, focus becomes null and the player
  shows its empty state.
- Rename and move rebase the focused path and tab locus just as they already
  rebase tabs. Deleting the focused trace follows the close fallback above.
- On workspace restore, focus is reconstructed from the validated saved active
  panel and active tab. Directory selection starts empty.
- Activating a node-only Coin opens a temporary signed-trace tab so the player,
  author gestures, and gold tab indicator still share one subject.

### Author gesture routing

Replace selection-sensitive target resolution with one resolver over
`UiFocus` and the current replay mode.

- Focused writable file: Step, Send, and Attest use the existing delivery
  policy against that file.
- Focused Coin: Step is disabled; Send and Attest target the exact immutable
  Coin node.
- Focused folder: explicit Step is disabled because folder Steps are caused by
  structural or tag changes under the protocol; Send and Attest target the
  folder's exact current node when available.
- Null focus: all author gestures are disabled.
- Historical replay projection: Step, Send, and Attest are disabled in the
  primary author bar; Fork is offered. Exact historical Send or Attest can be
  added later as an explicit history context action, not an ambiguous primary
  gesture.

Cmd/Ctrl+S must call the same focused target resolver as the Step button. No
gesture may read `DirectorySelection` or choose a panel by searching for a
selected path.

### Trace player routing

- The player subscribes to `UiFocus.trace` only.
- Changing focus stops playback, tears down the previous projection, resets the
  playhead, and loads the newly focused trace.
- While loading, focus and both gold indicators remain stable; the player shows
  loading or error state for that subject.
- A file loads its revision chain.
- A Coin loads its immutable trace shape. The player may collapse to a
  single-node state when there is no timeline to scrub.
- A folder loads its folder timeline, including membership and buffered panel
  occupancy observations already represented by protocol focus deltas.
- Scrubbing changes the moment shown, not `UiFocus.trace`.
- During folder replay, historical child panels are projections of the focused
  folder. They do not acquire gold merely because the replay makes one visible.
- Clicking a projected child's title is an explicit focus transition. It exits
  folder replay, focuses that child trace, opens or activates its live tab, and
  loads the child's player shape.
- Returning the player to the live head exits historical projection and
  restores author gestures for the focused live subject.

### Protocol boundary

No wire change is required. Existing protocol `focus` deltas continue to record
foreground panel occupancy and continue to drain only on structural folder
Steps. `UiFocus` itself is not serialized as a new event and never creates a
Step. Opening and focusing tabs may naturally produce the existing buffered
mount and unmount observations.

Directory selection is entirely press-local UI state and is never serialized.

### Migration shape

1. Introduce `UiFocus`, `DirectorySelection`, and pure focus transition helpers.
2. Make panel and directory gold styling derive from `UiFocus`.
3. Split directory activation from modifier selection and context-menu
   operation targeting.
4. Route Step, Send, Attest, Cmd/Ctrl+S, and palette availability through the
   focused target resolver.
5. Route the player and replay loading through `UiFocus.trace`.
6. Stabilize folder replay so projected children cannot silently steal focus.
7. Remove selection-driven target fallbacks such as `opTargetPanel()` once all
   consumers have migrated.
8. Rename any remaining ambiguous `selection` variables to distinguish
   directory selection, editor range selection, and replay playhead state.

### Verification cases

- File, Coin, and folder directory activation each open and focus one tab.
- A single gold directory bar and single gold tab top always agree.
- Duplicate tabs show gold only on the clicked tab instance.
- Modifier multi-selection leaves focus, author gesture availability, and the
  loaded player unchanged.
- Right-click and drag operate on the correct set without moving focus.
- A normal click clears stale operation selection.
- Step, Send, Attest, and Cmd/Ctrl+S target the same focused file.
- Coin and folder gesture matrices match the rules above.
- Focusing each trace kind changes the player shape and resets the playhead.
- Folder replay keeps the folder focused while historical children move across
  projected panels.
- Clicking a projected child exits folder replay and focuses the child.
- Historical projection disables author gestures and enables Fork.
- Closing, moving, renaming, deleting, and restoring layouts preserve or fall
  back from focus deterministically.
- Player loading and relay errors do not silently change focus.
- Closing and reopening during an audition restores the validated focused tab
  and leaves directory selection empty.
- Existing protocol focus-buffer tests continue to prove that UI observations
  never mint Steps by themselves.

## Open Questions

- Whether an exact historical Send or Attest deserves a dedicated replay
  context action after the first hiring pilot.
- Whether folder replay should eventually move into a dedicated composite
  replay stage, Approach C, after reviewers use the current projection model.
- Whether audition traces should be candidate-owned by default or provisioned
  under an agency-controlled workspace while retaining a candidate signing key.
- What minimum evidence a reviewer needs in the first read-only audition link:
  raw event detail, summarized phases, or both.

## Success Criteria

### Interaction correctness

- No Step, Send, Attest, or player load reads directory selection as its target.
- Every focus transition produces matching directory and tab focus indicators.
- Focus remains singular through duplicate tabs, folder replay, and restored
  layouts.
- All focus and replay edge cases above have regression coverage.

### Product validation

- Five content leads watch an uncoached replay of the same sample audition.
- At least three can identify one meaningful candidate decision from replay
  that the final document alone did not reveal.
- At least two ask to use the workflow with a real finalist or paid freelancer.
- Candidates can explain what is recorded before starting, and no candidate
  mistakes the workflow for covert surveillance or AI detection.
- A candidate can stop and resume during the 24-hour window without losing
  draft work, focus, or provenance evidence.

## Distribution Plan

The first pilot uses the existing desktop press for candidates and a local or
shared read-only replay flow for reviewers. Do not build broad ATS integration
before observing the pilot. Existing application release and verification
pipelines cover distribution during this phase.

The next commercial distribution decision depends on pilot behavior:

- If candidate setup is the bottleneck, prioritize a no-install audition press.
- If reviewer setup is the bottleneck, prioritize a signed read-only web link.
- If workflow administration is the bottleneck, prioritize reusable briefs and
  ATS handoff only after the core evidence proves useful.

## Dependencies

- Current multi-panel and folder replay behavior in `apps/client/src/App.tsx`.
- Existing `TraceRef`, panel layout, replay timeline, step policy, and protocol
  focus-buffer modules.
- Existing folder tab representation.
- Existing gold focus styles, which should be reassigned from mixed
  selection/focus semantics to singular focus only.
- A follow-on implementation plan should inspect and preserve the extensive
  uncommitted work already present on `codex/headless-press`.

## The Assignment

After the focus implementation is verified, run one paid take-home audition
with a 24-hour submission window. Have a content lead review the result and its
replay without coaching. Ask the reviewer to narrate what they believe each
replay signal means before explaining Zine. Record every mismatch between their
interpretation and the intended model. That session is the first real demand
and usability test.
