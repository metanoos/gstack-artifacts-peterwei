## Problem Statement

Zine has a substantial implemented product and two approved designs for an
AI-permitted writing-audition workflow, but it has no direct demand evidence.
No agency has described a costly hiring failure that Zine would have prevented,
no hiring owner has committed to a pilot, and no candidate has completed an
uncoached audition for a real buyer.

The startup question is therefore not whether Zine can record a richer writing
process. It can. The question is whether an agency that repeatedly hires
freelance writers finds finished samples too weak to judge a candidate once AI
is permitted, and whether process evidence changes a consequential hiring
decision enough to justify a new workflow.

The narrow hypothesis is:

> A content agency running an AI-permitted, paid writing audition will value a
> replay of the candidate's sources, model use, revisions, and acceptance
> decisions because the final sample alone no longer reveals enough judgment.

This is a hypothesis to falsify, not a product claim.

## Demand Evidence

### Direct evidence

None yet.

- No prospective buyer has paid, committed time, or changed a hiring workflow.
- No firsthand incident established that missing process evidence caused a bad
  hire, material editing work, client-quality failure, or avoidable uncertainty.
- The first buyer and workflow were inferred from category research and product
  reasoning.

### Category evidence

- Contentellect publicly recruits freelance writers through an application and
  a 1,000-word test article before onboarding. This supplies a concrete
  workflow to investigate, not evidence that Contentellect wants Zine:
  https://www.contentellect.com/careers/
- Contentellect's older hiring write-up names identity, outsourcing, quality,
  and consistency as concerns, while describing calls, offers, onboarding, and
  first assignments rather than inspectable process evidence:
  https://www.contentellect.com/hiring-and-onboarding-a-writing-team/
- Columbia Business School summarized research finding that generative AI made
  evaluators 4–9 percent less able to distinguish expertise in written pitches:
  https://business.columbia.edu/research-brief/impact-of-generative-ai-on-hiring-and-startups
- Current freelance-hiring guidance recommends short, job-relevant paid tests,
  structured rubrics, and sometimes live demonstrations. That supports testing
  a paid audition but does not establish that agencies will adopt Zine:
  https://www.ziprecruiter.com/hiring/how-to-hire/freelance-writing

The first-principles insight is that Zine should not try to make a test
"AI-proof." If the role permits AI, the more useful signal may be how the
candidate exercises judgment with AI. The interviews must test whether hiring
owners agree after describing their own workflow, without being led to that
answer.

## Status Quo

The researched composite is portfolios, application forms, writing samples,
test articles, interviews, Google Docs, editor feedback, and an initial client
assignment. Contentellect's current public page says applicants complete a
1,000-word test article; its older process article describes a Skype call,
immediate freelance offer, onboarding, and then a first project. The apparent
difference is a reason to ask about the current workflow, not to choose one
version as authoritative.

The interviews must reconstruct one recent hire from start to finish:

1. Where the candidate came from.
2. Which portfolio or application signals mattered.
3. Whether a writing test was used, whether it was paid, and why.
4. What AI use was allowed, forbidden, disclosed, or ignored.
5. Who reviewed the result and under what rubric.
6. What the reviewer still could not determine from the finished work.
7. What a bad decision cost in editing time, client risk, missed deadlines,
   replacement effort, or foregone candidates.

Until a hiring owner supplies that account, the status quo remains unverified.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

### First prospect

The first prospect is the current Head of Operations, or equivalent owner of
writer recruiting and onboarding, at Contentellect. The role must be verified
before outreach because ownership and titles may have changed.

### Comparison prospects

Recruit at least two more small-to-mid-sized content agencies that:

- repeatedly hire freelance writers;
- use test assignments or first-project trials;
- serve clients for whom writing quality and reliability affect retention;
- operate remotely enough that observation cannot rely on an in-person room;
- have a named operator accountable for the writer bench.

### Narrowest wedge

One real, paid, AI-permitted take-home audition for one freelance writer. The
agency supplies a realistic brief containing no live client material and its
own evaluation rubric. The candidate uses Zine for the audition, Sends and
Attests one exact result, and the agency reviewer evaluates the final output
before and after inspecting the process replay.

The pilot is deliberately concierge. Founder assistance, manual setup, and a
free Zine pilot are acceptable. The agency must still pay the candidate for the
audition; founder subsidy would not validate the agency's willingness to run a
fair paid test.

## Constraints

- No new product feature is authorized before the discovery gate passes.
- The first 20 minutes of each interview reconstruct the last real hire without
  showing Zine or asking leading questions about provenance.
- A Zine walkthrough appears only after the interviewee has named their current
  workflow, uncertainty, and cost.
- Zine must not claim to detect AI, prove human authorship, verify identity, or
  determine whether a candidate deserves employment.
- The pilot explicitly permits AI and evaluates judgment with AI. It is not a
  covert anti-cheating exercise.
- Pilot material contains no live client secrets or regulated personal data.
- Candidates receive clear consent, retention, deletion, and reviewer-access
  terms before starting.
- A candidate may decline or withdraw from the experimental process and submit
  through the agency's normal channel without penalty. Zine setup proficiency
  is not scored unless the job itself requires that tool, accommodations must
  be honored, and a Zine failure cannot count against the candidate.
- The pilot uses the existing press, model operations, Send, Attest, locator,
  verification, and replay paths. Missing operational packaging may block a
  pilot, but it does not justify speculative product scope.
- External AI use that Zine cannot observe must be disclosed as an evidence gap,
  not silently treated as captured process.

## Premises

1. The immediate problem is not provenance in general. It is whether AI has
   weakened finished writing samples as evidence of candidate judgment for one
   agency workflow.
2. Contentellect's public 1,000-word test is a discovery anchor, not demand
   evidence.
3. Zine shows inspectable process evidence. It does not detect cheating or
   prove human authorship.
4. The real competitor is the agency's existing application, test, interview,
   and onboarding process.
5. If that process does not cause costly uncertainty or bad decisions, Zine has
   no wedge here.
6. The existing product and approved designs are sufficient for discovery; no
   additional feature is needed before interviewing buyers.
7. A paid audition is a hypothesis that must be confirmed with agencies rather
   than assumed.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: One interview, one teardown

Interview Contentellect's hiring owner once, reconstruct the workflow, then
test a short process-replay walkthrough.

- Effort: small; roughly 3–4 human hours.
- Risk: medium because one person can create a false positive or false negative.
- Completeness: 5/10.
- Reuses: Contentellect's public process, the current press, and the two prior
  audition/onboarding designs.

### Approach B: Three interviews, then one concierge pilot

Run the same discovery protocol with three agencies. Proceed to a manually
supported real audition only if repeated pain and one behavioral commitment
clear a written gate.

- Effort: medium; roughly 10–15 active human hours across three to six elapsed
  weeks, excluding the chosen agency's pilot-scheduling latency.
- Risk: low-to-medium because recruitment is hard, but evidence quality is much
  stronger.
- Completeness: 10/10.
- Reuses: the current desktop press, existing model and provenance operations,
  replay, the prior focus/onboarding designs, and the normal release path.

### Approach C: Blind process-replay comparison

Show an agency reviewer two similar-quality outputs with different creation
processes, ask for an initial ranking, reveal the replays, and measure whether
the ranking or confidence changes.

- Effort: small-to-medium; roughly 4–6 human hours.
- Risk: high because a constructed example may manufacture delight without
  revealing demand.
- Completeness: 6/10.
- Reuses: the current replay surface and deterministic lesson material.

## Recommended Approach

Use Approach B: three interviews followed by one gated concierge pilot.

The workflow is an evidence ladder:

```text
15 qualified prospects
        |
        v
5 personalized first-wave messages
        |
        v
3 completed workflow interviews
        |
        v
Gate 1: repeated costly uncertainty + real upcoming paid audition
        | pass                         | fail
        v                              v
1 concierge pilot                 stop or change wedge
        |
        v
final-output score -> normal evidence packet -> Zine replay
        |
        v
Gate 2
   | PASS: repeat use/payment
   | SUPPORTING: introduction/case-study permission
   | FAIL: rejection, invalid comparison, or excess burden
```

### Stage 0: Prospect preparation

Build a list of 15 agencies. Record the agency, current hiring owner, evidence
that it hires freelance writers, public test/trial behavior, contact route, and
why it fits. Send five personalized messages in the first wave and continue
until three qualified interviews are complete.

Use a three-to-six-week recruitment window, separate from the 10–15 hours of
active work. Follow up once after four business days and once after another
seven. Expand through the full 15-prospect list in five-message batches rather
than treating five cold messages as sufficient to produce three interviews.

The outreach asks for help understanding the agency's latest freelance-writer
hire. It does not lead with blockchain, Nostr, signed traces, AI detection, or a
product demo.

### Stage 1: Three workflow interviews

Use the same 30-minute protocol for every interview:

1. "Tell me about the last freelance writer you hired, starting before the job
   was posted."
2. "Where did candidates enter, and what eliminated most of them?"
3. "What did the writing test tell you that the portfolio did not?"
4. "Was the test paid? Why or why not?"
5. "What AI use was allowed, and how did that affect evaluation?"
6. "What could you still not tell from the finished sample?"
7. "Tell me about a writer who looked strong during hiring but failed later."
8. "What did that miss cost your team or clients?"
9. "What do you inspect when two samples are equally polished?"
10. "Do you have an upcoming writer audition where this uncertainty will recur?"

Do not demo Zine during the first 20 minutes. In the final 10 minutes, reflect
the interviewee's workflow in their own words and show only the smallest replay
needed to test whether process evidence answers a question they already named.

Before outreach, define the evidence terms:

- A **qualified interview** is with someone who personally participated in at
  least one freelance-writer hire in the prior 12 months, can recount it
  end-to-end, and owns or materially influences the test and pilot decision.
- The **same material uncertainty** means two interviewees independently map to
  the same predeclared code—quality prediction, AI/tool judgment, identity or
  outsourcing, source reliability, or revision discipline—and each names a
  concrete hiring, editing, client, or time consequence.
- A **consequential question** is one whose answer changes a rubric score,
  advance/reject decision, interview question, or stated confidence threshold.
- An interview compliment, abstract concern about AI, feature suggestion, or
  willingness to see a demo is not a coded pain signal.

Use one evidence-capture template and lock the codebook before the first
interview. Preserve the interviewee's words alongside the assigned code so the
founder cannot manufacture convergence after the fact.

### Gate 1: Permission to pilot

Assess the evidence after three interviews. Proceed only when all of these are
true:

- three qualified interviews are complete;
- at least two independently describe the same material uncertainty in their
  own words;
- each repeated problem has a concrete cost or consequential decision;
- at least one agency has an upcoming audition and commits to run it through a
  concierge pilot;
- the agency agrees the audition will be paid and AI-permitted;
- the desired value is evaluating judgment, not covert AI detection,
  surveillance, or identity proof.

If zero qualifying costly pains appear, every stated need is disallowed
detection/surveillance, or no interviewee has a plausible upcoming audition,
stop after three. Do not reinterpret compliments, requests for a deck, or
general concern about AI as demand. Continue to a maximum of five only when at
least one qualifying costly pain exists but a second converging account or live
commitment remains unresolved. Stop or change the wedge after five unless two
independent accounts converge and the remaining gate conditions pass.

### Pilot readiness gate

Before scheduling a candidate, close every readiness item:

| Item | Owner | Evidence required | Pass rule |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hiring owner and workflow | Founder + agency | Named decision owner and current process map | Owner confirms the map in writing |
| Candidate path | Founder | Clean-persona install/write/Send/Attest/full-chain-transfer test | No developer tools, security bypass, or founder coaching in the core flow |
| Reviewer path | Founder + reviewer | Clean-persona receive/verify/replay test in desktop press | Locator opens, `historyComplete === true`, and the reviewer completes replay without developer tools |
| Full process closure | Founder | Expected event manifest and remote fetch report | Every expected source, model-operation, revision, final, and attestation event is retrievable with valid ids and signatures |
| Minimum observability | Founder + agency | Rehearsed safe brief and trace checklist | Every core source, model contribution, substantive revision, and final acceptance is represented |
| Normal evidence packet | Agency | Locked list of everything normally reviewed | Same packet can be shown before Zine replay without omitting material ordinary history |
| Candidate safeguards | Agency | Written payment, fallback, accommodation, withdrawal, and no-penalty terms | Candidate consents before setup |
| Data handling | Founder + agency | Completed inventory, access list, storage map, retention date, and deletion plan | No unresolved secret, personal-data, or relay-retention issue |
| Outbound network | Founder | Relay inventory, Send fanout test, and OTS behavior test | The pilot relay is the only configured external event destination; OTS is disabled or its irreversible public digest submission is explicitly accepted |
| Failure rehearsal | Founder | Completed outage/failure dry run | Every core failure has a normal-channel fallback and invalidation rule |

The primary reviewer path for this pilot is the desktop press receiving a
portable locator and verifying/replaying the trace. The raw trace may be an
audit aid, but it is not the primary human review surface. A no-install verifier
remains deferred.

Remote review uses a temporary ACL-protected clearnet relay with TLS and a
dedicated pilot database. The candidate press's NODE key is the relay owner;
the named reviewer's NODE key is a read-only peer. Do not authorize the
candidate NODE merely as a writer: desktop trace events are signed by AUTHOR
identities, while the relay writer role may publish only events signed by the
authenticated writer key. Owner authentication permits the candidate press to
publish those valid AUTHOR-owned events without weakening another user's access
because the database is pilot-specific. This is a controlled pilot service, not
a claim that private outbound onion reads or a portable Tor bundle work today.
Delete the relay database and credentials at the retention deadline and record
completion. Same-machine review is not an acceptable substitute because it
would avoid the remote handoff the wedge depends on.

### Stage 2: One concierge pilot

1. The agency chooses one real upcoming audition, supplies a safe brief and its
   normal rubric, locks the normal evidence packet, names one reviewer, and pays
   the candidate.
2. Before the audition clock starts, the founder helps the candidate install
   and verify the existing press, connect an allowed model if needed, and
   complete a dry run with disposable material.
3. During the audition, the candidate uses Zine for the durable artifact and
   follows the minimum-observability protocol. Every external source relied on
   is cited or minted into the trace; every model-generated passage entering
   the final work comes through a Zine-recorded model operation; substantive
   revisions are Stepped; and final acceptance is Sent and Attested. If core
   sourcing, model use, revision, or acceptance occurs outside the trace, the
   comparison is invalid rather than merely "disclosed."
4. The candidate Sends and Attests one exact result. Because Send deliberately
   publishes only the selected final Step, it does not make ancestry available
   to the remote reviewer.
5. After Gate 1, use a consent-gated pilot transfer that runs on the candidate
   machine, authenticates to the dedicated relay as the candidate NODE owner,
   and republishes the exact existing signed event closure without re-signing or
   changing Send semantics. Before transfer, show the candidate a manifest of
   the final node, every ancestor to genesis, expected source/citation events,
   model-operation nodes, substantive revisions, trace-head metadata, and the
   Attestation. The candidate approves that exact manifest and destination.
   The pilot is blocked until this transfer path exists and is tested.
6. From a clean reviewer press, resolve the locator and require
   `historyComplete === true`. Compare the remotely fetched event ids and
   signatures with the approved manifest. Any missing or unexpected event
   invalidates the handoff before the reviewer sees replay.
7. The reviewer follows three locked stages. First, score the final output only.
   Second, inspect the agency's predeclared normal evidence packet and score
   again. Third, inspect Zine replay without coaching and score a final time.
   Record rubric score, confidence, questions, reviewer time, and advance/reject
   decision after each stage. If the agency normally relies on conventional
   version history and the pilot cannot preserve an equivalent history in the
   normal packet, choose another workflow rather than claiming a fair
   comparison.
8. During replay, record which moments the reviewer opened, what questions were
   answered, what new questions arose, and the reason for every changed score,
   confidence level, or decision.
9. Interview the candidate and reviewer separately within 24 hours. Ask what
   was confusing, burdensome, useful, or trust-eroding.

Lock the scoring form before the pilot. Record `advance`, `hold`, or `reject`
after each stage. Record confidence in that decision on a 1–5 scale:

1. a guess that the reviewer cannot yet defend;
2. tentative, with major unanswered questions;
3. moderate, enough to choose the next interview question but not act;
4. strong, requiring at most one named verification before acting;
5. sufficient to act without further evidence.

A confidence change means at least one anchored point with a written reason.
Do not treat movement toward `advance` as inherently better than justified
movement toward `reject`.

The burden budget is explicit: candidate setup and reviewer setup each take no
more than 20 minutes after the rehearsed dry run; each path requires at most one
support intervention before the timed core flow; reviewer replay takes no more
than 30 minutes; and neither core flow suffers a data-loss, verification, or
security failure. Exceeding a threshold is recorded as a pilot failure, not
edited out as concierge overhead.

### Candidate protection and data handling

- Provide a normal-channel fallback submission from the start. Withdrawal from
  Zine cannot reduce pay, change the rubric, or count against the candidate.
- Do not score installation speed, provider setup, or Zine proficiency unless
  those skills are explicitly job-relevant and disclosed before consent.
- Offer the same accessibility accommodations as the agency's normal audition.
- Let the candidate inspect the exact trace before Send. Require a manual
  secret and personal-data review before making it reachable. Present the
  full-chain transfer manifest separately and require explicit approval before
  ancestry leaves the candidate's local relay.
- Inventory only the brief, working draft, cited sources, prompts, model output,
  signed trace, final artifact, rubric scores, and research notes needed for the
  pilot. Keep resumes and unrelated hiring records outside Zine.
- Use an isolated pilot profile and access-controlled, non-public relay. Name
  every allowed viewer: candidate, one agency reviewer, and founder support only
  with candidate consent. For remote review, "non-public" means the temporary
  ACL-protected clearnet pilot relay above; it does not mean onion transport.
- Configure the candidate press with no write-enabled external relay other than
  the dedicated pilot relay and verify Send fanout before the audition. Every
  Step currently attempts to submit its event-id digest to a public
  OpenTimestamps calendar. Because that digest is an outbound, irreversible
  disclosure even though it does not contain the document, default to disabling
  the optional OTS overlay for the pilot. If no supported switch exists, Gate 1
  may authorize the smallest tested pilot configuration needed to suppress it.
  Otherwise block the pilot or obtain explicit consent and remove any promise
  of complete network deletion.
- Set a written retention deadline, defaulting to 30 days after the agency's
  decision. Give the candidate an export and confirm deletion from the pilot
  relay and participating devices. Explain before consent that copies already
  exported by authorized reviewers and signed events sent beyond the isolated
  relay may not be retractable; do not make the latter reachable in this pilot.
- Record withdrawal, export, deletion requests, deletion completion, and any
  exception in a pilot data log.

### Operational failure matrix

| Failure | Preflight | Recovery and candidate fallback | Pilot result |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Provider/model outage | Fixed probe before clock | Pause clock, retry once, then normal-channel submission | Invalid if model use was part of the brief |
| Local relay or sidecar failure | Step/Send/receive dry run | Preserve local work, pause clock, retry after repair | Invalid if exact trace cannot be verified by deadline |
| App crash or lost connectivity | Crash/reopen and offline rehearsal | Reopen, verify trace head, extend clock by outage duration | Invalid on data loss or ambiguous head |
| Send or Attest failure | Disposable signed handoff | Submit normal final file; never penalize candidate | Invalid because reviewer cannot inspect exact committed result |
| Locator, signature, or replay failure | Clean reviewer acceptance test | Review normal evidence packet only | Invalid; do not substitute a founder demo |
| Incomplete or mismatched history closure | Manifest/fetch comparison with `historyComplete === true` | Stop before review; repair transfer and regenerate the fetch report | Invalid if any expected signed event remains unavailable |
| Secret or unintended personal data | Manual pre-Send review | Stop, revoke credentials, isolate/delete reachable copies, notify parties | Invalid and handled as a privacy incident |
| Unexpected relay fanout or OTS submission | Relay/OTS recording test | Stop before real content; remove the extra relay or disable the optional anchor | Blocked until behavior matches consent and retention terms |

The agency retains the hiring decision. Zine supplies evidence but never emits
a candidate score, recommendation, or automated employment verdict.

### Gate 2: Evidence of pull

Classify Gate 2 explicitly.

**PASS — earns another product cycle:**

- the replay answers at least one consequential question that the final file
  and the agency's locked normal evidence packet did not answer;
- the reviewer can explain the useful evidence without protocol coaching;
- the agency asks to use Zine for another real audition or agrees to pay for a
  defined continuation;
- candidate setup and use are acceptable enough that the agency will impose
  the workflow again;
- neither party mistakes Zine for AI detection or a verdict about the writer.

A changed score is informative but not required; greater justified confidence,
a better interview question, or a decision not to hire can also be valuable.
Repeat use or payment is required for PASS.

**SUPPORTING SIGNAL — does not pass alone:** a qualified introduction to another
hiring owner, permission to publish an anonymized case study, or a request for a
future feature.

**FAIL:** an explicit rejection, no repeat request, a request only for AI
detection/surveillance, candidate harm, an invalid comparison, or burden beyond
the predeclared budget. Record the rejection as evidence; do not place it on the
success path.

## Open Questions

- Does Contentellect still use the public 1,000-word test, and is it paid?
- Is the hiring owner's actual pain quality prediction, identity/outsourcing,
  AI policy, editing cost, client risk, or something else?

- Will the agency pay the candidate for the audition?
- Which external AI actions, research, or edits remain outside Zine's evidence,

- What retention and deletion terms will both candidate and agency accept?

Installation, reviewer delivery, minimum observability, candidate safeguards,
and data handling are readiness blockers above, not questions that may remain
open when the audition starts.

## Success Criteria

### Discovery

- Three interviews with named owners of freelance-writer hiring.
- At least two independent accounts of the same costly uncertainty.
- At least one detailed recent bad-hire, editing, client, or decision example.
- One committed upcoming paid, AI-permitted audition.
- A signed Gate 1 memo containing the locked codebook, coded evidence, verbatim
  support, dissenting evidence, and the proceed/stop decision.

### Pilot

- One candidate completes and submits a real audition through Zine.
- The reviewer evaluates the final result before seeing replay.
- The three-stage comparison shows replay answering at least one consequential
  question not answered by the final file or the agency's locked normal evidence
  packet.
- Candidate and reviewer can each describe what Zine proves and does not prove.
- The agency requests a second real use or agrees to pay for a defined
  continuation. A qualified introduction is supporting evidence only.
- Candidate and reviewer setup stay within 20 minutes each, require no more than
  one pre-flow support intervention, and the reviewer completes replay within
  30 minutes without founder coaching.

### Stop conditions

Stop or change the wedge when any of these occur:

- three interviews produce zero qualifying costly pains, only disallowed
  detection/surveillance demand, or no plausible upcoming audition;
- when the predeclared extension condition applies, five qualified interviews
  still fail to produce both convergence and a live pilot commitment;
- agencies want only AI detection, surveillance, or identity proof;
- no agency will pay candidates for the test;
- process replay does not affect questions, confidence, or decisions;
- installation and workflow burden outweigh the evidence for both sides;
- interest disappears when asked for an upcoming real audition.

## Distribution Plan

No public release or new distribution artifact is required for discovery.

For the concierge pilot, restrict support to one platform already covered by
the existing Tauri bundle path. Produce a clean bundle through the current
release instructions, test it on a clean machine, and complete candidate setup
before the paid audition clock begins. The founder may provide hands-on setup,
but the candidate and reviewer must perform the core writing and review flows
without coaching.

Run two separate clean-persona acceptance tests before recruitment reaches the
candidate: candidate install/write/Step/Send/Attest/approve-manifest/transfer,
then reviewer install/receive/verify-full-history/replay through the desktop
press. Preserve the commands, versions, event manifest, elapsed time, support
interventions, and failures as the distribution evidence for the readiness
gate.

The acceptance environment includes the temporary ACL-protected clearnet relay
described above. Test TLS, candidate-owner authentication, reviewer read access, locator
portability, relay retention controls, absence of unintended external relay
fanout, full-chain transfer, `historyComplete === true`, manifest equality, and
the chosen OTS-disabled or explicitly consented behavior. Do not depend on
outbound onion reads or the repository's placeholder Tor bundle.

If installation requires a development toolchain, disabling security controls,
or unreviewed credentials, the pilot is blocked. Do not hide that friction with
a founder-operated demo. Public release automation, cross-platform packaging,
and a no-install verifier remain downstream work gated on demand.

## The Assignment

Within seven days, identify the current owner of writer hiring at Contentellect
and four comparable agencies, then send five personalized requests for a
30-minute conversation about their most recent freelance-writer hire. Ask for
the interview, not a product reaction. Do not send a Zine deck or demo link.

The first message should make the request concrete:

> I am researching how content agencies evaluate freelance writers now that AI
> can make finished samples look equally polished. I saw that your team uses a
> writing test. Could I spend 30 minutes learning how your most recent writer
> hire actually worked? I am not asking you to buy anything, and I will not demo
> a product unless it helps answer a problem you raise.

## What I noticed about how you think

- You answered "C" twice when asked for direct demand and firsthand pain. You
  did not promote category research into evidence just to protect the idea.
- When the target stayed abstract, you said "search it up for me." That moved
  the session from "agencies" to one public 1,000-word test and one accountable
  operating role.
- You chose the three-interview evidence ladder over the faster single interview
  and the more theatrical blind demo. That choice prioritizes evidence strong
  enough to stop the project, not merely evidence that makes the product look
  good.
- The live repository shows that you are already building. The discipline now
  is to let three hiring owners determine whether more building is deserved.
