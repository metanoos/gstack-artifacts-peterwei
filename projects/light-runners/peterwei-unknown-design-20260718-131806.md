## Problem Statement

The existing Light Runners prototype is a technically complete location-based AR trail game, but its rules do not yet produce a sharp multiplayer party experience. A run currently ends on collision and is scored through distance, speed, route beauty, and proximity. Those systems reward several different behaviors without giving nearby friends one instantly readable objective.

The redesigned game should make a park feel like a real-world, three-dimensional game of Snake. Players move through an AR volume, collect spatial objectives, grow lethal trails, hunt the leader, crash, respawn, and leave a shared neon artifact behind. The rules must remain legible in one sentence and work for 3 to 8 friends without depending on stranger density.

## What Makes This Cool

The same physical route has three lives:

1. **Weapon:** during the match, the live tail is lethal geometry that can trap its owner or another player.
2. **Social chaos:** Lumen Gates pull players together, leader bounties create temporary alliances, and an optional referee can deliberately throw Gates into funny locations.
3. **Art:** when the clock ends, every route becomes non-lethal light art in a shared Afterglow.

The defining image is a group of friends standing inside a faint projected hemisphere while neon trails twist through the real park. Ground players see half-buried Gates as glowing hemispheres. Flying participants see the same Gate volume as a complete orb. The game is not displayed on top of a map; it appears to inhabit the world.

Core sentence:

> Enter Lumen Gates to score and grow your tail. Avoid every live trail, including your own. Most Lumens when time expires wins.

## Constraints

- First great session: 3 to 8 friends who meet in one place and start together.
- Unity 6000.4.x, iOS 14+, Android API 26+, AR Foundation 6, Photon Fusion 2, and Supabase remain the platform stack.
- Anonymous identity and six-character friend-room codes remain. No social graph is required.
- The host controls Lightfield radius, Lumen Gate radius, live-tail radius, Gates per player, and time limit. Player collision radius is fixed at 2 m for the first playable and is not a host or designer control.
- The match must degrade safely when AR tracking, GPS, altitude, networking, or a referee disappears.
- Movement play must be usable with brief glances. Critical information needs spatial audio, haptics, and screen-edge indicators.
- There is no gameplay speed limit and no transport-mode classifier. Any sensor-qualified movement uses the same swept-path rules. Active drivers and active pilots must not operate the game; passengers may compete under the universal rules, and the app treats this as a safety policy rather than trying to infer who is in control from speed.
- Flight is part of the coordinate model and data model from day one, but the first playable milestone is ground-only. Aerial participation unlocks only after measured vertical alignment meets the same collision-quality bar as horizontal alignment in a controlled test profile.
- The first playable uses a shared visual Anchor Beacon at the Lightfield center. GPS alone is not accurate enough to arbitrate Snake-like collisions.
- The current workspace has no Git metadata, so automated build and release infrastructure is not yet available.

## Premises

1. The Lightfield is a true hemispherical play volume, not a flat map polygon.
2. There is one universal ruleset across movement types. Separate car, runner, and flight modes would dilute the game.
3. One resource is enough. Passing through a Lumen Gate awards one Lumen and increases maximum live tail length.
4. A longer tail is stronger and more dangerous to its owner because self-collision is enabled after a short emergence grace.
5. Crashes cause a short respawn rather than elimination. A normal player drops one Lumen; any player tied for the lead drops two, capped by their current Lumens.
6. Dropped Lumens become temporary pickups. A crash therefore creates a local fight rather than silently editing the scoreboard.
7. The primary presentation is full-screen AR. A small radar, FPS-style directional indicators, spatial audio, and haptics provide tactical awareness.
8. Only live match tails cause collision. Completed paths persist as non-lethal Afterglow art.
9. An ordinary match needs no referee. Referee mode is an optional spectator and Gate Director role; it sends commands to the match host but never becomes rules authority merely by being the referee.
10. The new match rules belong in a pure, testable match core. GPS, AR, networking, persistence, and UI are adapters around it.
11. Tail radius is one host-selected, authoritative match setting shared by every player. Lumens change tail length, never thickness.
12. Player collision radius is a fixed 2 m rules constant for the first playable. It stays separate from tracking uncertainty and is not exposed beside the visible tail-radius control.

## Landscape Awareness

Current GPS-running games commonly use background tracking, territory ownership, and asynchronous competition. Running Empire explicitly records with the screen locked, while Stride converts routes into persistent territory and filters vehicle-speed activities out of competitive rewards. That conventional pattern is sensible for fitness tracking but does not create the live proximity tension this project wants.

Lightfield deliberately diverges: it is a bounded, scheduled party match with a visible clock and live hazards. The safety implication is that phone interaction must be peripheral, not the main dexterity test. NHTSA treats any non-driving phone interaction as a crash risk, and pedestrian-distraction research found mobile gameplay to be especially cognitively demanding.

- [NHTSA distracted-driving guidance](https://www.nhtsa.gov/risky-driving/distracted-driving)

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Evolve the current prototype

Replace scoring and crash behavior inside the existing managers, add Gates and the Lightfield, and generalize trails from ground paths to 3D over time.

- Effort: M
- Risk: High
- Strength: quickest ground-player prototype and maximum code reuse.
- Weakness: old run-ending, full-trail, four-axis score, and 2D-collision assumptions remain distributed across the runtime.

### Approach B: New Lightfield match core

Keep the existing platform systems as adapters, but create a pure match engine that owns the Lightfield, clock, players, Gates, Lumens, finite 3D trails, collision, shatter, respawn, and results.

- Effort: L
- Risk: Medium
- Strength: one authoritative, deterministic, testable rules model for ground and flight.
- Weakness: more restructuring before the first complete multiplayer match.

### Approach C: Required referee device

Run the match core on a dedicated host phone or tablet that owns Gate spawning, collision rulings, scoring, shared origin, and a spectator view.

- Effort: XL
- Risk: Medium-high
- Strength: consistent rulings and a strong party/event display.
- Weakness: every ordinary match needs an extra device and host loss becomes a critical failure.

## Recommended Approach

Build **Approach B**, with the useful part of C available as an optional role.

The match runs in Photon Fusion Host Mode. In a normal room, the room creator is the Host and holds match authority while still playing. A referee is an ordinary command client with permission to use Gate Director and safety pause controls. It does not own collision, scoring, or time. Referee absence never changes the player rules.

## Player Experience

### 1. Join

- A host creates a room and receives a six-character code.
- Friends join through the existing anonymous friend-room flow.
- Lobby shows player names, sensor readiness, AR readiness, altitude capability, and optional referee role.
- An **active player** is a non-referee participant who is Ready, sensor-qualified, and locked into the countdown roster. The active roster accepts 3 to 8 players.
- A room holds at most 10 connections: up to 8 active players, one referee, and any remaining slots as spectators. An unready participant or anyone joining after countdown spectates until the next round. Referees and spectators never affect Gate density.
- The ground milestone does not expose aerial participation. Later, players who fail altitude qualification remain ground-only rather than being excluded.

### 2. Tune the Lightfield

Host copy:

> Set the boundary. Dial in the chaos.

Host controls:

| Control | Recommended default | Initial range | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Lightfield radius | 120 m | 30-150 m in ground alpha | Hemispherical radius and maximum height; up to 1,000 m only after separate validation |
| Lumen Gate radius | 10 m | 3-20 m in ground alpha | Spherical collection radius; up to 50 m only after separate validation |
| Live-tail radius | 2 m | 1.5-4 m in 0.5 m steps | Radius of the cylindrical trail tube; the collision envelope adds the frozen player-head radius |
| Gates per player | 0.50 | 0.25-1.00 in ground alpha | Decimal slider in 0.25 steps; up to 2.00 only after load testing |
| Time limit | 6 min | 3-10 min in ground alpha | Whole-minute steps; 1-30 min only after drift validation |
| Referee | Off | On/Off | Separate participant role; never required |

The settings UI always previews the derived count:

> 6 players x 0.50 = 3 active Lumen Gates

It translates the selected tail radius into the actual hit rule with a live-bound preview:

> {selected tail radius} m tail + 2 m fixed player = {selected tail radius + 2} m head-to-tail collision distance

At the default tail setting this reads:

> 2 m tail + 2 m fixed player = 4 m head-to-tail collision distance

The 2 m player term is fixed for the first playable. Only the visible tail radius is adjustable, avoiding host-selected combinations that preserve head-to-trail distance while silently changing head-to-head collision and spawn clearances.

The Gate count is fixed from the player count at countdown:

```text
activeGateCount = max(1, ceil(playersAtStart * gatesPerPlayer))
```

`playersAtStart` is the locked active-player roster, excluding the referee and spectators. Automatic Gates are capped at 16. Manual referee Gates have a separate cap of `min(4, playersAtStart)`, for an absolute maximum of 20 live Gates.

Settings are cross-validated rather than accepted independently:

- `gateRadius <= 0.20 * lightfieldRadius`;
- `headToTrailCollisionCm = tailRadiusCm + playerHeadRadiusCm` and every trail-clearance rule derives from it;
- the derived count must fit the Lightfield with the configured separation rule;
- the host UI narrows invalid slider ranges in real time and blocks countdown if the seeded placement simulation cannot fill every automatic slot;
- settings freeze when countdown begins and never change during a round.

Collecting or expiring an automatic Gate schedules a replacement. Referee bonus Gates sit outside the automatic budget.

### 3. Calibrate

- The ground alpha uses a supplied 40 cm square high-contrast `ARTrackedImage` Anchor Beacon with a printed forward arrow. The Host places it flat at the chosen center, records the arrow-to-north yaw from a five-second compass median with reported accuracy at or below 10 degrees, and publishes that yaw with the WGS84 center.
- Each device follows `Unseen -> Collecting -> Qualified -> Stale -> Recalibrating`. Collecting requires 30 normally tracked marker poses over three seconds. Component samples beyond three median absolute deviations are rejected; at least 24 must remain. Median translation and circular-mean yaw become the device transform only when translation RMS is at most 0.25 m and yaw RMS at most 3 degrees.
- Readiness also requires GNSS sample age at most one second, reported horizontal accuracy at most 5 m, AR tracking Normal, and the computed shared-frame uncertainty at most 1.5 m. A player who cannot qualify can spectate instead of delaying the group.
- The room has one immutable `coordinateFrameVersion` per round. Each successful scan or re-scan increments that device's `deviceFrameVersion`; every sample carries both and the Host rejects older versions. Becoming Stale phases the player and guides them back to the beacon for a new three-second scan.
- If fewer than three active players qualify, countdown stays blocked. The ground alpha also blocks a site whose calibration samples vary by more than 2 m vertically from the beacon plane; uneven-terrain support is later work.
- After scanning, players spread out while still non-lethal. Countdown unlocks only when every active head is at least 10 m from another active head and 8 m inside the shell; the radar gives each player an outward setup arrow.
- The first shared ground alpha is validated on flat, open ground, but the product does not require every point in a Lightfield to be reachable by every participant. Players obey real-world access, traffic, and property rules; the match does not certify or model them. There is no hidden terrain mask: an inaccessible automatic Gate simply expires after 45 seconds and is replaced. Gate radius and Gates per player are the host's accessibility controls, while map warnings remain supplemental.
- Device qualification uses a separate four-phone harness: once per minute for six minutes, every phone localizes the same test marker at five field positions. The p95 of all pairwise reported-position separation must be at most 1.5 m. If the target phones cannot meet that bar, live trail collision does not ship unchanged.

### 4. Countdown

- All active tails are cleared.
- Players begin SpawnProtected at their actual calibrated positions; this physical-movement game never teleports a player's head to a virtual spawn.
- Automatic Gates materialize with a three-second telegraph.
- The Lightfield shell becomes visible and pulses 3, 2, 1.

### 5. Match

- Passing through a Gate grants one Lumen and immediately increases maximum live tail length.
- The tail grows behind the player's accepted movement path and dissolves from its oldest end once it exceeds the current length cap.
- Every live tail is the union of capsules: each centerline segment is dilated by the host-selected `tailRadiusCm`, producing a cylinder with hemispherical end caps, and adjacent capsules overlap at shared vertices. That full tube is lethal to every player, including its owner, except for the newest emergence-grace segment.
- Leaving the Lightfield shatters the player.
- A shattered player drops Lumens, loses the active tail, waits eight seconds while moving Phased toward open space, and returns from their current safe physical position in SpawnProtected state.
- The current leader is crowned in the HUD and in off-screen directional indicators.
- Most Lumens when the configured clock expires wins.

### 6. Results and Afterglow

- Live hazards immediately become non-lethal.
- The finished sculpture preserves the round's selected tail radius, so Overview and Walk Inside show the same tube thickness players navigated during the match.
- Afterglow opens in **Overview**, a top-down whole-Lightfield view that supports pan, zoom, rotation, and player-trail selection.
- A persistent **Overview / Walk Inside** toggle switches to full-screen AR without rebuilding the replay. **Walk Inside** anchors the same non-lethal trails to the original Lightfield so players can physically move around and through the finished sculpture.
- Selected players and a world-space focus pivot persist across the switch. Overview centers its camera on that pivot; Walk Inside highlights it and points toward it while the AR camera remains controlled by the device's physical pose. The two modes are views of one shared Afterglow package, never separate recordings.
- Walk Inside is guaranteed immediately after the round while the match calibration is still valid. A later revisit may restore precise placement only when the original Beacon remained fixed or a separately qualified persistent/surveyed anchor exists. Replacing a removable Beacon from ordinary GNSS is not precise enough; in that case the app keeps Overview available and labels any optional AR placement as approximate.
- Results show winner or co-winners, Lumens, crashes, Gate collections, and leader bounties.
- The first playable keeps the result screen factual. Expressive awards such as Trail Artist and Boldest Line are a later presentation layer after the core metrics prove trustworthy.
- Run It Back preserves the room and settings but reseeds Gate positions.

## Rules Model

### Lightfield geometry

The Lightfield is defined in a shared east/up/north frame whose origin is the scanned Anchor Beacon:

```text
center = (0, 0, 0)
valid position when:
  y >= -2 m
  length(position - center) <= lightfieldRadius
```

The lower tolerance prevents ordinary ground jitter from causing a boundary shatter. The ground milestone still simulates all coordinates in 3D, but only qualified ground players join. A later aerial profile may use the full upper hemisphere. Crossing either the curved shell or the base-circle edge triggers boundary shatter.

Boundary feedback has three bands:

1. **Awareness:** at 15 m from the shell, it becomes visible and a soft directional tone begins.
2. **Danger:** at 7 m, a screen-edge warning, repeating haptic, and stronger shell pulse begin.
3. **Crossed:** the first Host-accepted Active sweep outside the volume produces a boundary shatter.

For fields smaller than 60 m, the two warning widths scale to 12.5% and 25% of radius. A player whose current uncertainty is too large to distinguish these bands becomes Phased instead of risking a false shatter.

Authoritative first-playtest constants:

| Tuning | Default | Designer range |
|---|---:|---:|
| Simulation rate | 20 Hz | Fixed for first playable |
| Position sample rate | 10 Hz | Fixed for first playable |
| Maximum sample age | 0.4 s | 0.2-0.5 s |
| Maximum collision uncertainty | 1.5 m | 0.5-2 m, device-validated |
| Player collision radius | 2 m | Fixed for first playable |
| Head-to-trail centerline distance | 4 m at defaults | `tailRadius + playerHeadRadius` |
| Head-to-head centerline distance | 4 m fixed for first playable | `2 * playerHeadRadius` |
| Collision broad-phase microsegment | 4 m | Fixed geometry-processing bound |
| Emergence grace | 8 m | 4-20 m |
| Trail vertex spacing | 0.75 m | 0.25-2 m |
| Respawn delay | 8 s | 3-15 s |
| Spawn-protection time | 3 s | 1-5 s |
| Spawn-protection travel | 8 m | 4-15 m |
| Dropped-Lumen radius | 2 m | 1-3 m |
| Active trail segment cap | 512 per player | 128-1,024 |
| Afterglow point cap | 2,048 per player | 512-4,096 |
| Live entity caps | 20 Gates, 64 drops | Fixed hard caps |
| Ordered event batch cap | 256 per tick | Fixed hard cap |

Except for the host-selected tail radius and values derived from it, these are designer configuration rather than additional host controls. The player collision radius is stricter: `playerHeadRadiusCm = 200` is a fixed first-playable rules constant, not a tuning range. All durations are stored as integer simulation ticks, all match positions and distances as integer centimetres, and all seeded placement uses PCG32 plus checked signed-64-bit squared-distance comparisons. The configured coordinate bound is `abs(axis) <= 100,000 cm`, covering the later 1 km field. There is no displacement or speed rejection inside that bound. Long accepted sweeps are divided into contiguous collision microsegments of at most 400 cm whose endpoints map to the canonical 16-bit sweep coordinate; this is deterministic geometry processing, not a gameplay speed cap. Collision predicates have overflow tests at the coordinate and microsegment bounds.

Deterministic numeric contract:

- PCG32 uses the published XSH-RR 64/32 algorithm, match seed as initial state, and stream `54`; every subsystem owns a documented draw range so adding a visual draw cannot perturb spawning.
- Ground Poisson candidates use integer rejection sampling in the bounding square, then squared-radius and squared-separation tests; no floating square root participates. Future 3D candidates use the equivalent bounding cube rejection.
- Bearing search uses a frozen 32-entry Q1.15 unit-circle table. Drop orientation uses `SplitMix64(stableDropId) mod 1024` into a frozen 1,024-entry Q1.15 circle table. Both tables are generated once into source and covered by hashes.
- Arc length uses floor integer square root of centimetre squared distance. Partial-segment clipping and odd-tick interpolation use rational numerator/denominator math with round-half-to-even. The 15-degree turn test compares the squared dot product with a frozen Q2.30 `cos^2(15 degrees)` constant; a negative dot always counts as a turn.
- Continuous contact existence is decided before time quantization. Sphere, capsule-feature, and Lightfield-boundary sweeps produce a bounded set of integer quadratic intervals; exact discriminant signs and feature-domain tests preserve tangencies and intervals narrower than one 16-bit step. Integer root isolation brackets the earliest real entry time, then assigns `q = floor(65,535 * tEntry)`, deliberately rounding toward the earlier contact. The event remains valid even when the position sampled exactly at `q` is just outside the volume; `q` orders an already-proven continuous contact rather than defining collision on a time grid. Repeated primitive hits deduplicate to the earliest `q`, and equal `q` values use cause priority and stable ID. Golden tests compare existence and quantized entry against an arbitrary-precision reference over random, tangent, narrow-graze, and boundary cases. No platform `Mathf`, locale, wall clock, or unordered collection affects authoritative output.

At roster lock, `FrozenMatchConfig` converts the chosen seconds/metres into named integer fields and hashes the result. At defaults it contains `tailRadiusCm = 200`, the fixed `playerHeadRadiusCm = 200`, `headToTrailCollisionCm = 400`, `headToHeadCollisionCm = 400`, `collisionMicrosegmentCm = 400`, `respawnTrailClearanceCm = 1,200`, `spawnExitTrailClearanceCm = 800`, `activeHeadClearanceCm = 1,000`, `shellClearanceCm = 800`, `countdownTicks = 60`, `inputDelayTicks = 4`, `respawnDelayTicks = 160`, `shatterEffectTicks = 20`, `safeReentryTicks = 20`, `spawnProtectionTicks = 60`, `spawnProtectionTravelCm = 800`, `recoveryBlockedTicks = 240`, `recoveryPulseTicks = 100`, `recoveryPulseRadiusCm = 1,500`, `recoveryInnerRadiusCm = 300`, `dropTelegraphTicks = 15`, `dropActiveTicks = 300`, `autoReplacementTicks = 40`, `gateTelegraphTicks = 60`, `autoGateActiveTicks = 900`, `manualGateActiveTicks = 600`, and `gateBlockedExpiryTicks = 100`. Derived clearances preserve their default safety margins: `respawnTrailClearanceCm = headToTrailCollisionCm + 800`, `spawnExitTrailClearanceCm = headToTrailCollisionCm + 400`, and `activeHeadClearanceCm = headToHeadCollisionCm + 600`. Allowed designer or host changes update their frozen fields before countdown; state transitions reference the fields by name. No first-playable path may change `playerHeadRadiusCm`.

`tailRadiusCm` serializes as one of exactly `{150, 200, 250, 300, 350, 400}`, while `playerHeadRadiusCm` serializes as exactly `200`; any other player-radius value invalidates the first-playable config. One canonical config-derivation routine computes every collision and clearance field from the base host/designer settings plus that fixed player-radius constant. Room creation, join, snapshot restore, and Host migration all rerun that derivation, compare every serialized derived field plus the config hash, and reject the config on any mismatch. Clients render trail and Afterglow thickness only from the validated frozen `tailRadiusCm`; they never trust an independently supplied visual radius.

The 64-drop capacity check is speed-independent and deliberately conservative:

```text
minimumShatterCycleTicks = respawnDelayTicks + 1
requiredDropCapacity = playersAtStart * 2
                       * ceil((dropTelegraphTicks + dropActiveTicks)
                              / minimumShatterCycleTicks)
require requiredDropCapacity <= 64
```

The added tick is the minimum possible SpawnProtected interval before Active can be scheduled, even when one accepted sweep travels beyond `spawnProtectionTravelCm`. The formula assumes the worst legal two-Lumen drop on every minimum-length shatter cycle. Designer combinations such as a very short respawn and a long drop lifetime remain valid only when this check fits the fixed 64-drop pool.

### Lumen Gate geometry and spawning

A Lumen Gate is one spherical trigger volume:

- Ground placement centers the sphere on `y = 0`; only the reachable upper half participates in boundary/terrain placement and renders as a hemisphere, while collection is the head-center distance to the Gate center.
- Aerial placement renders the complete sphere.
- Spawn altitude is selected from the occupied player-altitude bands. A ground-only lobby therefore receives only ground Gates.
- Gate collection uses shared match coordinates and ignores local AR-rendering drift.

For a ground Gate, the “Lightfield minus radius” test is exactly `center.y = 0` and `center.x^2 + center.z^2 <= (lightfieldRadius - gateRadius)^2`; the deliberately buried lower half is ignored. With flight enabled, a full-orb Gate instead requires `center.y >= gateRadius` and `length(center) + gateRadius <= lightfieldRadius`.

Automatic spawning uses a deterministic match seed and a bounded Poisson-disc sampler. A candidate must:

- lie within the Lightfield after subtracting Gate radius;
- remain at least `max(2 * gateRadius, 0.08 * lightfieldRadius)` from other Gates;
- remain at least `gateRadius + 5 m` from every connected non-spectating player head whose pose is currently sensor-qualified;
- keep its full trigger plus `headToTrailCollisionCm` clear of every current lethal trail, so collecting never requires entering an already-lethal tube;
- not overlap the previous four accepted positions for that automatic slot;
- fall within an occupied altitude band.

The lifecycle is explicit:

```text
ReplacementPending (2 s) -> Telegraphing (3 s) -> Active (up to 45 s)
Active -> Claimed | Expired -> ReplacementPending
```

In tick fields, an automatic claim/expiry at `r` schedules placement at `r + autoReplacementTicks`, activation at placement plus `gateTelegraphTicks`, and exclusive expiry at activation plus `autoGateActiveTicks`.

At countdown, automatic slots start at Telegraphing so they become active on the first match tick. An uncollected automatic Gate relocates after 45 seconds, preventing an inaccessible placement from starving the board. The authority makes 128 placement attempts, then relaxes only Gate-to-Gate separation down to `1.25 * gateRadius`. Boundary, spawn, and packing rules never relax. Failure leaves the slot pending and shows the host a specific invalid-density warning.

A Gate claims on an Active player's swept entry or, when Telegraphing changes to Active, an already-overlapping Active head. Each Gate can award exactly once. Contention follows the authoritative tick-order rule below.

Phased, Stale, Shattered, and Disconnected heads neither reserve Gate clearance nor collect. A valid sweep may enter and claim several distinct Gates or drops in one tick; every object resolves independently and still awards once. Temporary loss of qualified players never changes the roster-locked automatic Gate count.

Trail clearance is checked again when Telegraphing would become Active. A blocked automatic Gate returns to ReplacementPending; a blocked manual Gate becomes Cancelled and frees its slot. At tick-end step 9, an unclaimed Active Gate increments `blockedByTrailTicks` when the just-resolved live geometry intersects its trigger plus collision corona and resets it to zero when clear. Reaching `gateBlockedExpiryTicks` schedules expiry for the next tick-start; a claim in the reaching tick wins first. This permits brief tactical blocking without leaving an impossible objective for its full lifetime.

### Lumens and tail length

Lumens are both score and spatial power. Initial playtest curve:

```text
maxLiveTailMeters = min(120, 12 + 6 * currentLumens)
```

- `12 m` gives a zero-score player a visible tail without making the first seconds lethal.
- Each Lumen adds `6 m` of reach.
- `120 m` caps both memory and runaway arena control.

These are playtest defaults, not brand promises. The match core exposes them as designer configuration, not host-facing settings.

Tail **length** remains player-specific and grows with Lumens. Tail **radius** is one host-selected match setting shared by everyone and frozen at countdown. Players therefore compete under identical thickness while earning different reach.

The active tail and the Afterglow path are separate data streams:

- **Active tail:** pruned by arc length to the current live cap; used for collision and replication.
- **Afterglow path:** never participates in collision, but the Host canonically assembles it from accepted sweeps. During Running, it emits append-only raw parametric-sweep chunks containing ordered endpoints, `q`-truncated shatter endpoints, and discontinuity tags; confirmed raw chunks are immutable. At Results, the Host deterministically simplifies the complete raw stream to at most 2,048 visible points per player. Every discontinuity remains either a visible span boundary or a metadata-only omitted span, so simplification never invents a connecting line. The Host then publishes a separate immutable final package containing the `LightfieldDefinition`, frozen tail radius, anchor identity/origin/yaw, player-tagged polylines, factual round summary, point/discontinuity counts, final chunk manifest, and SHA-256 content hash. Overview and Walk Inside consume only this same verified final package. Milestone 3 uploads the Host-finalized package for participant persistence.

The Host reliably broadcasts numbered raw Afterglow chunks plus their hashes during the round and retransmits missing chunks by manifest entry before final simplification. Clients validate sequence, previous-chunk hash, endpoint/discontinuity counts, the final-package manifest, and the final package hash before marking the replay Complete; after three seconds they may show an explicitly Partial replay while retries continue. For milestone 4 migration, each match snapshot stores only the confirmed raw-chunk frontier and hashes while peers cache the addressed chunks. A replacement Host discards chunks beyond the restored frontier and requests missing confirmed raw chunks by hash, so it reconstructs one authoritative append-only history rather than merging divergent or already-simplified paths.

Final simplification first budgets whole discontinuity spans. If two endpoints per non-empty span fit under the per-player point cap, every span remains visible. Otherwise it always retains the first and last span, then retains remaining spans by descending accepted arc length with start `runningTick` and raw index as stable ties until the two-endpoint minimum fits. Omitted spans become metadata-only entries containing tick range, accepted arc, bounding box, point/discontinuity counts, and raw-chunk hash; the UI says how many fragments are hidden, and neither renderer draws a bridge across them. The remaining point budget is distributed among retained spans by accepted arc length with deterministic largest-remainder ties. Inside each retained span, the simplifier preserves endpoints, ranks removable interior points by checked signed-128 squared perpendicular error, removes the lowest-error point, updates its two neighbors, and repeats to that span's budget; original raw-point index breaks equal-error ties. The Host includes simplifier schema/version and omitted-span manifest in the package hash, and golden fixtures verify identical output on Editor and IL2CPP.

`AfterglowViewState` stores selected players and a selected world-space focus pivot independently from either renderer. Switching views therefore changes navigation, not geometry or result state. Overview renders the complete package in a top-down rotatable scene and works away from the original site. Immediate Walk Inside reuses the still-valid match calibration. Persistent precise Walk Inside requires an unmoved Beacon or separately qualified anchor; otherwise it is unavailable or explicitly approximate. Neither AR form has collision, score, or boundary interaction.

On respawn, active geometry restarts at zero and grows behind the player until it reaches the cap implied by remaining Lumens. This creates a comeback window without inventing a second resource.

### Movement sampling and trail collision

The device samples local AR pose at 10 Hz and attaches current GNSS, marker-alignment, tracking, and uncertainty metadata. Per-sample uncertainty is the adapter estimate `sqrt(scanPoseRms^2 + trackingResidualRms^2 + driftBudget^2)` defined under Shared Coordinates; a trail segment stores the larger value of its endpoints. GNSS corrects slow drift; it does not provide the moment-to-moment collision path. Discontinuities from GPS loss, pause, explicit AR relocalization or frame-version change, respawn, or host migration never create connecting segments. Displacement magnitude alone never creates a discontinuity.

Canonical input pipeline:

1. Fusion runs at 20 Hz. A client emits one sample on each even local Fusion network tick with `authorityEpoch`, `playerId`, `networkTick`, increasing `inputSequence`, both frame versions, centimetre position, uncertainty, `arPoseAge`, and `gnssAge`.
2. On every entry to Running, the Host records `(epoch, networkTickBase, runningTickBase)`. Within that uninterrupted Running span, `inputRunningTick = runningTickBase + (sample.networkTick - networkTickBase)`. Pause, Countdown, and migration discard competitive samples; resume records a new base. A future sample above `hostNetworkTick + 1`, an old epoch, or a negative mapped Running tick is rejected.
3. The Host resolves mapped input tick `I` only when its corresponding Host network tick reaches `networkTickBase + (I - runningTickBase) + inputDelayTicks`, creating the default 200 ms arbitration delay. A sample received after that cutoff, with non-increasing sequence, wrong frame version, `arPoseAge > 0.4 s`, `gnssAge > 1 s`, uncertainty over 1.5 m, or an axis outside the configured bound is rejected.
4. Bootstrap after initial start/resume/migration clears the old buffer, waits for the first three valid 10 Hz samples, uses their component median as a non-swept head anchor, and begins arbitration only afterward. At steady state, an even tick uses the component median of previous/current/next samples; the odd tick between two filtered samples uses their integer midpoint with round-half-to-even. End-of-span samples are discarded rather than extrapolated.
5. If the next sample is absent at cutoff, hold the previous head without extending its trail. A later sample never bridges that held gap; it starts after a discontinuity and phases the player for normal safe re-entry. Eight ticks (0.4 seconds) without a valid AR pose also phases the player; stale GNSS separately makes the uncertainty estimator unqualified after one second.
6. Each non-held resampled tick produces one parametric swept head interval from the previous head. The Host accepts it at any speed when its frame versions, ages, tracking state, uncertainty, and coordinate bounds qualify. A long interval remains one continuous gameplay sweep and one potential trail segment; transient at-most-400-cm microsegments exist only for broad-phase index traversal. High speed alone never phases the player, rejects a sample, or splits the persisted trail.
7. Head sweeps use every resampled tick. Each persisted `TrailVertex` stores position plus monotonic `acceptedArcCm`. `trailVertexSpacingCm = 75` is a strict minimum difference in that cumulative distance between vertices, not sample rejection, chord length, or forced tessellation. A heading change greater than 15 degrees marks the current sample as the next preferred vertex but cannot violate the spacing minimum; sub-threshold accepted arc carries forward. Thus one long straight sample emits only its endpoint, while a genuine tracking or lifecycle discontinuity resets the local accumulator and inserts a discontinuity tag. Live-tail pruning and the segment-count proof use `acceptedArcCm`, never reconstructed chord length: config validation requires `ceil(maxLiveTailCm / trailVertexSpacingCm) + 2 <= activeTrailSegmentCap` at every designer-range extreme.

This pipeline is pure match input. Client timestamps cannot win a Gate or rewrite another player's order.

Collision is true 3D swept-volume collision, not the current XZ segment-intersection test:

- every accepted movement interval creates a head sweep and, when Active, a trail segment;
- every persisted trail segment renders and collides as a capsule: a cylinder of `tailRadiusCm` around its centerline plus hemispherical end caps; adjacent capsules union at shared vertices so bends have no cracks;
- a spatial index returns candidate trail segments;
- inclusive segment endpoints count;
- a head-to-trail collision occurs when 3D centerline distance is at most `headToTrailCollisionCm = tailRadiusCm + playerHeadRadiusCm`;
- a head-to-head collision occurs when two Active head sweeps pass within `headToHeadCollisionCm = 2 * playerHeadRadiusCm`, and both players shatter;
- a stationary Active head is still a collidable head for head-to-head contact, but is not a persistent wall;
- altitude is part of distance, so a later qualified flyer can pass above a ground trail when the volumes do not intersect;
- the newest 8 m of a player's own trail is visible but non-lethal to that owner; it remains lethal to other Active players;
- SpawnProtected and Phased players own no live trail; their displayed route is Afterglow only.

The broad phase traverses each at-most-400-cm transient microsegment in temporal order and returns only trail segments whose centimetre AABB, expanded by the frozen `headToTrailCollisionCm`, overlaps that microsegment's box. The configured field bounds one sweep to `ceil(2 * lightfieldRadiusCm / 400)` microsegments: 75 at the 150 m ground-alpha maximum and 500 at the later 1 km maximum, or at most 600 and 4,000 respectively across eight players on one input tick. Narrow-phase math subtracts the microsegment start as a local origin, so candidate relative coordinates are bounded by the overlapping boxes rather than the full 1 km field. A sweep-object pair produces at most one earliest contact event even if several microsegments or trail pieces find it. The published schema folds score changes, leader statistics, spawned drop IDs, and next lifecycle into their parent player/object resolution event rather than emitting unbounded sub-events. The roster-lock proof is `2 * players + 2 * (liveGates + liveDrops) + acceptedGateDirectorCommands + 1 <= 2*8 + 2*(20+64) + 8 + 1 = 193 < 256`; the final term is the optional match-level command/event. Config validation enforces this proof, and input sequencing admits at most eight Gate Director accept/cancel operations in one tick. Attempting to exceed a checked bound is a test/build failure, never a reason to reject fast movement at runtime. Checked signed-64-bit integer predicates and the canonical continuous-contact/`q` solver determine rulings; property tests cover every legal tail radius plus parallel, degenerate, endpoint, tangent, narrow-graze, radius-boundary, maximum-coordinate, and full-field-sweep cases.

Only heads and segments whose stored uncertainty is at or below 1.5 m can create or hit lethal geometry. Above that threshold, the player becomes Phased. “Deterministic” means the same integer match tick, quantized centimetre positions, ordered input sequence, and seed produce the same event log on every supported platform; the pure core's golden tests verify byte-identical events.

Each Running tick collides against an immutable snapshot of live trails that existed at tick start. Current-tick movement does not become lethal trail geometry until the next tick, preventing a head from hitting trail laid later in the same 50 ms interval. After contact resolution, a surviving Active player's full accepted sweep is appended to `SpatialTrail`; a shattered player's live tail is removed and no current-tick live segment is added. Afterglow receives a survivor's full sweep or a shattered player's sweep truncated at its earliest shatter `q`, so the finished route ends at the ruled crash point rather than beyond it. The live polyline is then clipped by `acceptedArcCm` to the tail cap based on the tick-start score; a partial boundary point is interpolated from the source sweep at the retained cumulative-distance boundary. The newest eight metres is clipped by the same metadata into an owner-only non-lethal grace view. Spatial chord length never controls retention or the 512-segment proof. A Lumen awarded in the tick changes the cap beginning next Running tick. Only Active players own `SpatialTrail`; every route shown for another state is Afterglow and never re-enters the live index.

Collision participation matrix:

| Head state | Own live tail | Other live tail | Other Active head | Gates/drops |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Active | Lethal after 8 m grace | Lethal | Mutual shatter | Collects |
| SpawnProtected | No | No | No | Cannot collect |
| Phased | No | No | No | Cannot collect |
| Shattered/Respawning/Spectating | No | No | No | Cannot collect |

### Shatter and dropped Lumens

The authority resolves the score state immediately before the shatter:

```text
dropCount = min(currentLumens, tiedForLead ? 2 : 1)
```

- Every player tied at the highest score counts as a leader.
- Score decreases immediately. Dropped Lumens telegraph for 0.75 seconds around the crash point, remain Active for 15 seconds, and can be collected by any Active player.
- Each pickup behaves like a Gate reward: one score and the associated tail-cap growth.
- Dropped Lumens never spawn outside the Lightfield. Boundary shatters project the crash point 3 m inside the shell before the drops materialize. Two-drop events use opposite points on a deterministic 2 m ring.
- A player at zero Lumens drops nothing.

Drops are reward volumes, not collision geometry. They may sit near a surviving trail, deliberately making the scramble dangerous. The shattered player's own live tail disappears before its drops telegraph.

```text
LumenDrop: Telegraphing (0.75 s) -> Active (up to 15 s) -> Claimed | Expired
```

A shatter at `r` schedules drop activation at `r + dropTelegraphTicks` and exclusive expiry at activation plus `dropActiveTicks`.

When a drop matures, every already-overlapping Active head is an entry at fraction zero. Contention then uses player ID; different drops still resolve independently by stable drop ID.

Each drop is a 2 m-radius spherical trigger with stable ID `(shatterEventSequence, dropIndex)`. In the ground profile, a hash of that ID chooses a horizontal angle; a one-drop event uses the corrected crash point, while a two-drop event uses opposite points on a 2 m ring. Each center is projected into the hemisphere shrunk by the drop radius and above the base plane, so its full trigger stays in bounds. The same Active-head sweep and uncertainty qualification used by Gates tests drop entry.

Each drop awards exactly one Lumen. A player may claim multiple overlapping drops in one tick; objects resolve by stable ID, while contention for each object resolves by swept entry fraction and player ID. The hard 64-drop budget is cross-validated against player count, drop lifetime, minimum respawn delay, and the one-tick minimum protection interval; an invalid designer config cannot start a match.

Shatter attribution uses the pre-tick state. Boundary and self-tail shatters have no hunter. A foreign trail credits its owner; if multiple trails are crossed in the same sweep, the earliest swept time wins, then the lowest stable player ID. In a head-to-head shatter, each participant receives credit if the other was a leader immediately before the tick. A leader bounty is a result statistic, not an extra Lumen.

### Respawn

The game cannot teleport a real person, so respawn happens at the player's measured physical position. During the eight-second Shattered/Respawning delay the player is non-interactive, may move, and receives an arrow toward open space. `RespawnDirector` evaluates 32 seeded horizontal bearings at 5, 10, and 15 m, rejects points outside the field, and points toward the candidate with greatest minimum game-hazard clearance. This arrow is not pedestrian navigation: it makes no claim that the physical route is clear, and the player must stop and choose a safe real-world path.

After the delay, re-entry at the current position requires:

- at least `respawnTrailClearanceCm` from every lethal trail centerline, which is 12 m at the default tail radius and grows one-for-one with a thicker tail;
- at least `activeHeadClearanceCm` from every Active player head, fixed at 10 m for the first playable;
- at least `shellClearanceCm` inside the Lightfield shell, 8 m at defaults.

Gate clearance is not required because SpawnProtected cannot collect, and becoming Active while already inside a Gate does not count as entry; the player must exit and re-enter.

If the hazard conditions fail, the player remains Phased and the open-space arrow updates each tick. Each shatter fixes one recovery anchor by projecting the crash point to `y = 0` and at least `recoveryPulseRadiusCm` inside the shell. Starting when the respawn delay ends, `blockedTicks` increments on every Running tick not spent in SpawnProtected; brief clearance resets `safeTicks` but not `blockedTicks`. At `recoveryBlockedTicks`, the qualified player may return within `recoveryInnerRadiusCm` of that in-bounds anchor to trigger one pulse.

The pulse begins during player-state step 4, before the tick-start trail snapshot and contact generation. Simultaneous pulses sort by recovering player ID and their spheres union. Step 4 converts existing live trail fragments inside `recoveryPulseRadiusCm` to Afterglow and removes them before step 5 captures the immutable collision snapshot; steps 6-8 suppress contact and reward claims in the union, and step 9 suppresses new live-trail emission there. Any other player inside or entering becomes Phased without score loss until leaving, closing their live tail. The recovering player must stay within `recoveryInnerRadiusCm`, inside the Lightfield, and qualified for all `recoveryPulseTicks`; failure consumes the pulse and returns them to Phased. At the exclusive pulse end, `respawnTrailClearanceCm`, `activeHeadClearanceCm`, and `shellClearanceCm` are rechecked. Passing schedules Active for the next tick with a zero-length tail; failing returns to Phased. The fixed anchor, visible warning, anti-camping rule, and one-use limit make this a liveness valve rather than a movable trail eraser.

Without the fallback, sensor qualification and all clearances must hold continuously for one second before the player enters SpawnProtected. Protection ends after three seconds or eight metres of accepted travel, whichever happens first, but only while the head remains at least `spawnExitTrailClearanceCm` from every lethal trail centerline, `shellClearanceCm` inside the boundary, and `activeHeadClearanceCm` from every Active head; loss of clearance returns the player to Phased. SpawnProtected and Phased players cannot score, shatter, or be shattered; their route is visible but does not leave a lethal trail. If either crosses the shell, it stays Phased and is guided inward without losing Lumens; only an Active player can boundary-shatter.

### Authoritative tick order

`runningTick` advances only after a complete Running pipeline, so pause freezes every gameplay timer. Initial and resume countdowns last exactly `countdownTicks` network ticks (60, or three seconds, by default) and do not advance `runningTick`. Automatic Gates created before the initial countdown carry `activateRunningTick = 0`.

At each 20 Hz boundary:

1. If `completedRunningTicks == timeLimitTicks`, finalize the prior confirmed state before accepting pause, Gate, movement, collision, or collection. Otherwise validate commands in sequence order with precedence `EndMatch > Pause > Resume > GateDirector`. Entering Paused stops the pipeline immediately. While Paused, only EndMatch or Resume is accepted; Resume enters the 60-tick countdown and closes old live tails as specified in the state model.
2. At tick start, advance lifecycle boundaries. `ReplacementPending` and `Telegraphing` mature when their scheduled tick equals `runningTick`; an Active object whose exclusive `expireRunningTick` equals `runningTick` expires before collection. Thus entry on `expireRunningTick - 1` wins, while entry on the expiry tick does not. Automatic, manual, and drop activation ticks are respectively scheduled from their explicit delays.
3. Resolve the canonical buffered movement input for this tick and update sensor/frame qualification.
4. Advance player states. Disqualifying transitions take effect now; counter-completed transitions into Active are scheduled for next tick, preventing the same sweep from being both protected and lethal.
5. Capture one immutable spatial snapshot of live trails as they existed at tick start. Current-tick path geometry is not in this snapshot.
6. Generate continuous head-versus-boundary, head-versus-snapshot-trail, and head-to-head contacts with the canonical 16-bit sweep coordinate `q`. Sort by `(q, causePriority, stableObjectId)`, where same-time priority is `Boundary, HeadHead, ForeignTrail, OwnTrail`. Walk the list: an unmarked boundary or trail contact marks that player; a head-to-head contact marks both only if neither was marked earlier. Snapshot trail geometry remains lethal for the rest of this tick even if its owner is marked.
7. Batch marked shatters and assign exactly one cause and at most one hunter to each. Any shatter anywhere in the tick prevents that player from claiming a Gate or drop in that tick, even if its entry fraction was earlier. Mutual head-to-head shatters remain order-independent.
8. Resolve Active Gate/drop overlaps and swept entries for remaining Active players. For each stable object, earliest `q` wins, then lowest stable player ID. One sweep may claim multiple distinct Gates and drops; every object still awards at most once.
9. Apply the score batch, leader/bounty statistics, claims, cancellations, and new Pending lifecycles. Remove shattered tails. Append surviving Active sweeps to live trails and append full or shatter-truncated sweeps to Afterglow, then clip live length and owner grace using the cached tick-start score. Update Gate block counters against this post-resolution geometry. New live segments become collision candidates next tick; score changes affect tail caps next tick.
10. Publish the bounded ordered event batch and state hash, increment `completedRunningTicks`, and advance to the next `runningTick`.

At the end of the round, highest Lumens wins. Ties go to fewest shatters, then most leader bounties. Players still tied are co-winners; no device-clock timestamp breaks a tie.

## Optional Referee: Gate Director

Referee mode is intentionally playful. It exists to create stories, not merely settle disputes.

The referee receives a rotatable 3D Lightfield view with:

- tap-to-place bonus Lumen Gates;
- drag-before-release for aerial altitude once the controlled-flight milestone is enabled;
- player, trail, boundary, and automatic Gate overlays;
- clock pause/resume for real-world safety interruptions;
- remove-last-manual-Gate control;
- spectator camera and results controls.

Manual Gate rules:

- Placement must be inside the Lightfield and at least `gateRadius + 5 m` from every connected non-spectating player head whose pose is currently sensor-qualified.
- Placement must remain at least `1.25 * gateRadius` from another live Gate.
- Placement must keep the trigger plus the frozen `headToTrailCollisionCm` clear of lethal trails.
- A Gate telegraphs for three seconds before becoming collectible.
- A manual Gate remains Active for 30 seconds or until collected, then frees its slot.
- Acceptance at `r` schedules activation at `r + gateTelegraphTicks` and exclusive expiry at activation plus `manualGateActiveTicks`.
- Manual Gates supplement automatic density and do not suppress automatic replacements.
- Before accepting a manual placement, the Host runs the same bounded fill simulation for every automatic slot, treating the candidate as occupied. A request that would prevent any current or next automatic slot from filling is rejected.
- “Place at will” means the referee may place whenever one of the `min(4, playersAtStart)` manual slots is free. The UI shows available slots before placement; an invalid request is rejected visibly rather than silently coalesced.
- A referee disconnect changes no authority. Existing manual Gates remain until their normal claim or expiry.
- Remove Last Manual Gate emits a `Cancelled` transition for the most recently placed live manual Gate and immediately frees that slot.

The Host may also play. Role and authority are orthogonal: a non-Host referee is a command client; if the room creator chooses to spectate and direct Gates, that device remains authoritative only because it is Host, not because it is referee. Losing that device follows Host-migration rules rather than the harmless referee-disconnect rule. The Host validates every Gate Director command against the current epoch, role permission, config, field, and manual-slot budget.

## AR, Radar, and Cues

### Primary AR view

- Full-screen camera feed.
- Live trails render the same capsule union used by collision: each centerline segment is dilated by the validated `tailRadiusCm` into a cylinder with hemispherical end caps, and adjacent capsules overlap at shared vertices. A translucent outer collision corona extends one `playerHeadRadiusCm` beyond the visible tube to the derived head-center threshold; a further shimmer shows current shared-frame uncertainty so the ruled danger is visible rather than hidden.
- Ground Gates appear as radiant hemispheres; aerial Gates as full orbs.
- The Lightfield shell is normally faint and becomes prominent near the boundary.
- Occlusion is visual only. Match rules do not depend on whether ARKit/ARCore believes a real object blocks a trail.

### Corner radar

The radar is a game instrument, not a detailed street map. It shows:

- Lightfield boundary;
- player heads and short tail silhouettes;
- Lumen Gates and dropped Lumens;
- vertical chevrons for objects above or below the player;
- the local player's heading.

It expands only before/after a round or while the match is Paused. OSM detail may appear in the expanded setup/results map but should not compete with live play.

### Screen-edge indicators

Every off-screen player receives an FPS-style directional indicator:

- arrow direction points toward the shortest screen-edge bearing;
- distance is shown in metres;
- `ABOVE`/`BELOW` appears when vertical separation is meaningful;
- the leader carries a crown;
- opacity falls with distance so eight players do not create permanent clutter.

Gate indicators use a different silhouette from player indicators. Boundary warnings always outrank both.

Vertical labels appear at 5 m of separation after subtracting declared uncertainty. The radar's expanded interaction mode is unavailable during Running regardless of movement speed.

### Audio and haptics

- Spatial hum from nearby live trails.
- Gate spawn chime from its bearing and altitude.
- Distinct pickup pulse for a Lumen.
- Accelerating boundary tone plus repeating haptic near the shell.
- Shatter burst and respawn countdown.
- Leader-change sting heard by the room.

The match remains understandable if the camera is pointed away from the action for several seconds.

## Match State Model

Replace the current global `Crashed` flow with match-level and player-level state.

Match states:

```text
Lobby -> Configuring -> Calibrating -> Countdown -> Running -> Results
                                            |          |
                                            v          |
                                          Paused ------+
                                          (Resume uses a fresh countdownTicks Countdown)
```

Three qualified players are required only to lock the initial roster and begin countdown. After Running starts, the clock and roster-locked Gate count continue when fewer than three players are Active, including when everyone is temporarily Phased; the Host may use safety pause but the match does not auto-pause on sensor state. If every active-roster participant is disconnected for 15 continuous Running seconds, the Host ends the round as **No Contest** with last standings but no winner. A reconnect before that threshold resumes against the unchanged roster and Gate budget.

Per-player states:

```text
Joining -> Ready -> SpawnProtected -> Active -> Shattered -> Respawning
                   |       ^             |                       |
                   v       |             v                       v
                 Phased ---+         Disconnected              Phased
Joining/Ready -> Spectating when excluded from the locked roster
```

Transition rules use Running ticks only:

| From | Trigger | Effective state and counters |
|---|---|---|
| Ready | Initial Countdown completes | SpawnProtected with `protectedTicks = 0`, `protectedTravel = 0` |
| Active | Sensor/frame qualification failure, app background, or disconnect | Phased/Disconnected immediately before geometry; close live tail and reset recovery counter |
| Active | Chosen contact cause | Shattered for `shatterEffectTicks`, then Respawning; re-entry evaluation begins `respawnDelayTicks` after the shatter event |
| Respawning/Phased | Qualified and normal respawn clearances hold | Increment `safeTicks`; reset to zero on any failure; at `safeReentryTicks = 20` schedule SpawnProtected for next tick |
| Respawning/Phased | Eligible one-use RecoveryPulse | SpawnProtected immediately for `recoveryPulseTicks` inside the recovery sphere |
| SpawnProtected | Qualified and all exit clearances hold | Increment `protectedTicks` and accepted `protectedTravel`; reset both and become Phased immediately on any failure |
| SpawnProtected | `protectedTicks >= spawnProtectionTicks` or `protectedTravel >= spawnProtectionTravelCm` | Schedule Active for next tick; re-check clearance at that boundary, then start a zero-length live tail |
| SpawnProtected/Phased | Outside shell | Phased, no drop or score loss; guide inward |
| Any live state | Results | Non-interactive result participant; all live geometry becomes Afterglow |

RecoveryPulse uses `recoveryPulseTicks` instead of the ordinary time/distance exit trigger; it still requires qualification at every tick but suppresses local hazards for the same interval. Pause freezes every listed counter. Disqualifying transitions take effect before the current tick's spatial snapshot; transitions into Active wait until the following tick, so one sweep is never both protected and lethal.

Pause behavior:

- App backgrounding, stale samples, excessive uncertainty, or invalid coordinate-frame data phases that player before the current Host tick's geometry. Their tail immediately stops growing and becomes non-lethal. Speed alone never changes interaction state.
- Any individual transition to Phased closes that player's live tail into non-lethal Afterglow. Refocusing requires fresh beacon/AR/GPS qualification plus one continuous second of safe clearance before returning to Active with a zero-length live tail. No trail segment bridges the gap.
- The Host or referee may request a safety pause. Pause takes effect at a tick boundary and freezes the match clock, Gate/drop telegraphs and expiry, respawn/protection timers, trails, and all score interaction.
- Devices keep observing sensors while paused but those positions do not enter the match path. Resume closes every pre-pause live tail into Afterglow, starts a three-second countdown, inserts a discontinuity for every player, and leaves each player Phased until qualification and clearance pass.
- Host loss also freezes simulation. Migration either restores one ordered snapshot epoch or ends the round as Connection Lost; a successful resume closes pre-loss live tails and protects re-entering players. Clients never continue competing under separate authorities.

## Architecture

### Pure match core

Create a Unity-independent `LightRunners.Match` assembly containing:

- `MatchConfig`
- `LightfieldDefinition`
- `MatchState`
- `PlayerRoundState`
- `GateState`
- `LumenDropState`
- `SpatialTrail`
- `MatchSnapshot`
- `MatchEvent`
- `GateSpawner`
- `CollisionResolver3D`
- `RespawnDirector`
- `MatchSimulation`

The core consumes tick-indexed, quantized player samples plus sequenced commands and emits deterministic events/snapshots. It does not call GPS, Photon, Supabase, Unity physics, AR Foundation, or UI.

### Shared coordinates

The current `CoordinateConverter` resets to each run's first GPS fix. That is insufficient for multiple clients to render one Lightfield consistently.

Calibration converts the beacon's WGS84 latitude, longitude, and ellipsoidal altitude through the WGS84 ellipsoid into Earth-centered/Earth-fixed coordinates (EPSG:4978), then rotates offsets into the Unity-friendly left-handed match axes `x = east`, `y = up`, `z = north`. The scanned beacon's physical center defines local `y = 0`; noisy absolute GNSS altitude receives a per-device bias and is used only for slow drift checks.

Calibration creates and distributes:

- a double-precision WGS84 Lightfield center;
- an Earth-centered/Earth-fixed to east/up/north basis and the Host-recorded beacon-arrow yaw;
- a per-device transform from scanned Anchor Beacon space into that shared basis;
- median ground altitude baseline;
- deterministic match seed;
- immutable room coordinate-frame version.

Devices transform local AR pose through the scanned-beacon transform and send quantized centimetre offsets from the common origin. Double precision is used only for the WGS84/Earth-centered conversion; the match core operates on bounded signed integers. GNSS/barometer residuals may adjust a device transform at no more than 0.10 m/s and 1 degree/s. This rate limits coordinate-frame correction, not player movement. A larger transform correction marks that device Stale, increments its device-frame version after re-scan, phases it, and creates a path discontinuity. Collision and rendering use the same shared-position estimate so the displayed hazard and the ruled hazard cannot silently diverge.

AR Foundation does not promise a positional covariance, so the adapter estimates rather than invents it. `scanPoseRms` is the square root of the mean squared translation residual from the accepted marker samples. For the ground profile, barometer/vertical residuals qualify the site but do not enter collision. Over the latest five seconds, horizontal `trackingResidualRms = sqrt(sum(w * |deltaARxz - deltaGNSSxz|^2) / sum(w))`, with `w = 1 / max(1 m, reportedHorizontalAccuracy)^2`; samples above 5 m accuracy or three median absolute deviations are excluded. `driftBudget` is a measured p95 metres-per-second rate times seconds since marker observation, loaded from a versioned, signed device-profile asset produced by the four-phone harness and keyed by device model, OS, and AR provider version. Unknown tuples yield infinity. Final uncertainty uses the stated root-sum-square, rounds upward to integer centimetres, and phases above 150 cm. AR Limited/None, fewer than 20 valid residual samples, or any missing term also yields infinity.

Phase recovery depends on cause:

| Cause | Recovery requirement |
|---|---|
| One packet gap/discontinuity with AR still Normal | Clear the input buffer, bootstrap three samples, then hold normal safe clearance for `safeReentryTicks`; no beacon re-scan |
| GNSS age/accuracy or residual failure | Twenty new valid residual samples plus `safeReentryTicks` |
| AR Limited/None over 0.4 s, app background, transform correction over limit, or device-profile/version change | Return to Anchor Beacon and complete Recalibrating before `safeReentryTicks` |
| Network reconnect under two seconds with unchanged AR/frame versions | Bootstrap plus `safeReentryTicks`; longer reconnect requires beacon re-scan |

The future aerial profile qualifies only when a rolling five-second window has vertical RMS uncertainty at or below 1.5 m, horizontal/shared-anchor residual at or below 1.5 m, sample age below 0.5 seconds, and uninterrupted AR tracking. Occupied altitude bands are deterministic 10 m bins; Gate spawning weights a band by its qualified active-player count. Loss of any threshold phases the player. Until milestone 5 sets `flightFeatureEnabled`, all band logic and aerial UI are disabled, every Gate is ground-mounted, and the match adapter projects accepted player positions to rules-plane `y = 0` after using raw vertical motion for sensor/site qualification. Lifting a phone cannot dodge a trail.

### Authority

- Lightfield changes the current transport from Fusion Shared Mode to Fusion Host Mode. The room creator is Host and therefore State Authority for the match object; clients provide movement input but never award their own Gates, Lumens, or shatters.
- Referee permission is a validated command capability on that match object. It never transfers State Authority.
- Match time is an integer Host tick. Client wall clocks never order competition events. Every client sample carries player ID and a monotonic input sequence; every command carries role, sequence, `authorityEpoch`, and command ID.
- In milestone 2, `EnableHostMigration` is off. Host loss freezes immediately and ends as Connection Lost after eight seconds, showing last-received standings without a winner. Host safety pause is already available locally; milestone 3 only adds referee permission and UI.
- Milestone 4 enables Fusion's Host Migration system with a two-second `Host Migration Snapshot Interval`. Photon Cloud selects the replacement Host and sends its selected peer the last stored state through `HostMigrationToken`; the game does not run a second custom election or client quorum.
- Every peer handles `OnHostMigration` by freezing presentation, shutting down the old `NetworkRunner`, creating a new Runner, and calling `StartGame` with the token. On the selected Host only, `HostMigrationResume` recreates the match `NetworkObject` from `GetResumeSnapshotNetworkObjects()`/`CopyStateFrom`, increments `authorityEpoch`, marks the departed Host player Disconnected, and rejects commands or samples from old epochs. Connection tokens restore stable player identity. The new Runner's Fusion tick has no continuity assumption: all input buffers clear, the restored `completedRunningTicks` remains authoritative, and the first post-countdown Running tick records a fresh `(epoch, networkTickBase, runningTickBase)` mapping and three-sample bootstrap.
- The migration object contains a complete `MatchSnapshot`: config, completed tick, PRNG state, all player/score/state counters, compressed active trails, Gate/drop state, last accepted input sequences, the last 64 command IDs, and each player's confirmed Afterglow chunk sequence/hash frontier. It needs no gameplay event-suffix replay. Photon stores only its latest snapshot, so a successful migration may rewind at most two seconds; the UI says so, discards Afterglow chunks beyond the restored frontier, closes restored live tails through a new discontinuity, and resumes through a three-second protected countdown. Missing confirmed chunks are requested by hash; divergent client geometry is never merged.
- Snapshot bytes are little-endian and begin with magic `LRMS`, schema version, payload length, `FrozenMatchConfig` hash, authority epoch, completed Running tick, and CRC32C. Player/Gate/drop IDs are explicit stable unsigned-64-bit IDs and never regenerated from Fusion object IDs. Trail point tags are `0 = signed-int16 xyz delta`, `1 = signed-int32 xyz absolute` when any delta overflows, and `2 = discontinuity`; every restored list validates count and bounds before use.
- The serialized game payload has a hard 40 KiB ceiling, leaving 8 KiB for Fusion/protocol overhead under a 48 KiB measured wire ceiling. Each trail stores one signed-32-bit centimetre point followed by signed-16-bit centimetre deltas and explicit discontinuities; 8 x 512-point trails consume under 25 KiB. Players/config, 20 Gates, 64 drops, PRNG, dedupe state, and the small Afterglow manifest frontier must keep the worst case under 40 KiB. Afterglow geometry chunks remain outside the migration payload and are content-addressed separately. Transport tests fail above 32 KiB/s normal replication per client, 256 KiB/s aggregate Host traffic, or 24 KiB/s average migration traffic including measured protocol overhead.
- If token restart or snapshot recreation fails within eight seconds, the round ends as Connection Lost with no declared winner.

Fusion documents Host/Server authority separately from per-object Shared Mode authority, and its Host Migration API says Photon selects the new Host, only that Host receives the stored snapshot, a new Runner must be created with the migration token, and game-specific state must be reconstructed. The design maps directly to those primitives rather than inventing referee-owned authority: [network topologies](https://doc.photonengine.com/fusion/current/manual/network-topologies), [NetworkObject state authority](https://doc.photonengine.com/fusion/current/manual/network-object), [Host Migration API](https://doc.photonengine.com/fusion/v2/manual/advanced/host-migration), [Host Migration sample](https://doc.photonengine.com/fusion/current/technical-samples/fusion-host-migration), and [authority-change reliability caveats](https://doc.photonengine.com/fusion/current/concepts-and-patterns/reliability-exceptions).

## Failure and Fairness Rules

| Condition | Behavior |
|---|---|
| Shared-frame uncertainty exceeds 1.5 m | Player phases: cannot collect, cannot collide, and leaves no lethal trail; no punishment |
| Valid high-speed or long movement interval | Accept one continuous swept path, subdivide it only for ordered collision processing, and apply the normal Gate, trail, and boundary rules |
| AR relocalization, frame-version change, or a missing-neighbor sample | Phase the player and mark a discontinuity; never draw a phantom segment across invalid tracking state |
| AR tracking lost | Phase the player and continue in radar/audio guidance until requalified; GPS alone does not arbitrate collisions |
| Altitude quality lost in aerial band | Phase player, warn, and return only after stable altitude; do not snap them through trails |
| Network disconnect | Phase player and make tail non-lethal; reconnect from the current snapshot if the round remains active |
| Fewer than three Active players after the round begins | Keep the clock, locked roster, and Gate budget running; sensor state alone does not auto-pause |
| Every active-roster participant disconnected for 15 Running seconds | End as No Contest with last standings and no winner |
| Match Host disconnect | Milestone 2 ends Connection Lost; milestone 4 uses Photon-selected Host plus migration token/snapshot, or ends after eight seconds |
| Referee disconnect | Authority is unchanged; automatic Gates continue and manual Gates follow normal expiry |
| No valid automatic Gate position | Relax Gate separation to its documented floor, never safety bounds; leave the slot pending and show the host why |
| Gate/drop contention | Earliest interpolated swept-entry fraction wins, then lowest stable player ID; client clocks are irrelevant |
| Tied final score | Fewest shatters, then most leader bounties; remaining ties produce co-winners |
| Player joins after countdown | Spectates until the next round; active player count and Gate budget remain fixed |

## Privacy and Physical Safety

- Keep anonymous UUID identity and no chat or social graph.
- The host is responsible for choosing a lawful, physically safe Lightfield location.
- Setup warns when radius intersects mapped roads or restricted areas, but the map cannot certify safety.
- Active movement never requires tapping a precise target.
- The match has no gameplay speed limit and no walking/running/vehicle classifier. Walking, running, rolling, riding as a passenger, and later qualified flight all use the same Gate, Lumen, tail, and collision rules.
- Active drivers and active pilots must not operate the game. Passengers may compete when doing so is lawful and does not distract the operator. The app cannot determine who controls a vehicle, so this remains explicit policy and onboarding copy rather than speed-based enforcement.
- Some Gates may be inaccessible from a particular road, path, mobility device, or altitude. That is an expected consequence of one universal spatial ruleset: hosts tune Gate radius and density, and an uncollected automatic Gate expires and relocates after 45 seconds.
- Poor sensor quality phases a player rather than killing them. The game must never punish uncertainty as if it were player error.
- The first playable keeps Afterglow participant-only. Public 24-hour visibility and longer geometry retention are later release decisions that require a separate privacy review.
- The one-time disclaimer must explicitly cover camera distraction, driver/pilot non-participation, passenger responsibility, traffic, terrain, local access rules, and location sharing.

## Success Criteria

### Rules comprehension

- Four new players can explain the win condition, self-collision, leader drop, and boundary shatter after one 20-second explanation.
- No player mistakes the Afterglow path for a live collision wall.

### Match quality

- A four-player, six-minute round creates at least three contested Gate or dropped-Lumen moments.
- Every player collects at least one Lumen under default density in 90% of test rounds.
- A player who crashes in the first minute remains meaningfully competitive.
- At least half of test groups choose Run It Back without prompting.

### Spatial correctness

- All clients agree on Gate collection, Lumen totals, shatters, and final results.
- All clients enforce the fixed `playerHeadRadiusCm = 200`, display the same frozen tail radius, and derive byte-identical head-to-trail, head-to-head, respawn, spawn-exit, and Gate-clearance distances plus broad-phase AABB expansion from them. The independent `collisionMicrosegmentCm = 400` processing bound remains fixed and produces identical traversal at every tail radius.
- Editor, iOS, and Android replay the same 10,000 seeded input fixtures into byte-identical ordered event logs without an integer-overflow assertion.
- Remote trail reconstruction stays within 0.25 m of the authoritative shared-coordinate path before sensor error.
- Four target phones maintain no more than 1.5 m p95 pairwise shared-frame error throughout a six-minute ground round.
- No phantom segment appears across pause, sensor dropout, respawn, or reconnect.
- Full-field valid sweeps at every coordinate extreme preserve ordered Gate, trail, head, and boundary contacts without overflow or speed-based rejection.
- On the minimum supported iOS and Android devices, a synthetic eight-player 150 m-alpha worst case completes the 20 Hz match tick under 50 ms p99, and the later 1 km/full-field sweep fixture must meet the same bar before that radius range unlocks. Optimization may change broad-phase traversal but never reject movement or alter contact ordering.
- Ten observed rounds at the minimum, default, and maximum tail radii produce no shatter where the ruled derived contact visibly misses the tube, collision corona, and uncertainty shimmer on the affected player's device.
- A stale or otherwise sensor-unqualified device becomes non-interactive within the specified 0.4-second window.
- Milestone-4 fault injection restores the Photon migration snapshot within eight seconds in 99% of 100 test runs, never merges divergent score, never exceeds two seconds of rollback, and respects the 40 KiB payload, 48 KiB wire, and traffic ceilings.

### Presentation and safety

- AR, radar, edge arrows, sound, and haptics communicate every critical state.
- A player can locate the nearest Gate and the leader without expanding the map.
- A player whose AR tracking drops for 15 seconds is safely Phased, can orient by radar/audio, and rejoins without a phantom segment.
- Observed players do not need to look continuously at the screen while moving.
- Afterglow opens in top-down Overview on every supported device and switches to immediate-session Walk Inside in under one second without reloading or changing trail geometry.
- Player selection and the world-space focus pivot survive every Overview/Walk Inside switch. The AR camera remains sensor-controlled, while the pivot is highlighted and directionally cued. AR trails remain non-lethal and, during the immediate session, stay within the normal shared-frame error budget.
- Away from the original Lightfield, Overview remains usable. A later precise Walk Inside is enabled only with the original unmoved Beacon or a separately qualified persistent anchor; any freely replaced AR placement is labeled approximate and is not measured against collision-grade alignment.
- Every completed round produces a hash-verified Afterglow package on all connected clients; missing chunks retry visibly, preserve discontinuities, and never create a geometry mismatch between the two views.

### Referee

- A referee can place a valid ground Gate in under three seconds; aerial placement is evaluated in the later flight milestone.
- All players see the same manual Gate materialize and become collectible at the same time.
- Referee disconnect does not interrupt the match.
